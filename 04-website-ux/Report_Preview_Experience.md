# Report Preview Experience

> The report is where the Intelligence Brain explains itself. It must feel like an **executive
> decision document** an operator could act on — not a dashboard to interpret, not a PDF to
> file, not a data dump to wade through. It is the payload of the diagnostic and the strongest
> single proof that Growmerce is an intelligence operating system.

It renders the Brain's VALUE → DECIDE stages (20) and the Pattern → Report mapping (19) into the
visitor's most important on-site moment.

---

## 1. The feeling we are engineering

> "This is a decision document. It tells me what's wrong, how sure they are, what it's costing
> me, and what to do first — and it's clear that they can actually do it."

A CEO reads a one-page decision memo, not a 40-tab dashboard. The report preview is that memo:
prioritised, evidence-backed, honest, and action-oriented.

---

## 2. Anatomy of a finding (the report's atomic unit)

Each finding is a recognition (19), now fully evidenced. It shows nine things — exactly the
fields the brief requires — in a scannable, decision-first layout:

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  FINDING        "Your discount lifts orders but erases margin."  │
│  EVIDENCE       discount on ~X% of orders; margin trend ↓ while   │
│                 revenue flat (the signals we used)               │
│  CONFIDENCE     Medium — "we strongly suspect; here's how we'd    │
│                 confirm with your order data"                     │
│  MISSING DATA   cost/margin per SKU would move this to High       │
│  IMPACT         margin recoverable: [range], not just revenue     │
│  EFFORT         low / medium / high                               │
│  PRIORITY       Now / Next / Plan  (with the reason for the order)│
│  RECOMMENDED    the specific first action                         │
│  GROWMERCE CAN  what we'd execute in Growth Operations            │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

| Field | Source in the Brain (20) / Patterns (19) |
|---|---|
| Finding | recognition / matched pattern |
| Evidence | Evidence Hierarchy (tiered signals) |
| Confidence | Confidence & Trust Model (4 bands, honest language) |
| Missing data | calibrated abstention / what would raise confidence |
| Impact | Revenue vs **Profit** Leakage Intelligence (separated) |
| Effort | Recommendation framework (effort/resources) |
| Priority | Opportunity Ranking + sequencing (Now/Next/Plan) |
| Recommended action | Recommendation engine (Quick Win first) |
| Growmerce can execute | wiring to Service Packages (11) + Growth Ops (12) |

The **"missing data"** and **"Growmerce can execute"** fields are deliberate: the first proves
honesty (and seeds the deep diagnosis), the second connects every finding to the paid service
without a hard sell.

## 3. The report structure (decision-first, not data-first)

```
1. THE HEADLINE DIAGNOSIS
   One sentence: the single most important thing happening (the top-priority finding).
   The "if you do one thing" line.

2. THE SYSTEM NARRATIVE
   2–3 sentences: how the findings connect (compound findings from the Interaction Model, 20).
   "You don't have three problems — you have one fragile growth model. Here's the shape of it."

3. THE PRIORITISED FINDINGS  (Now → Next → Plan)
   Each as the anatomy above. Sequenced with a stated reason, never a flat list.

4. WHAT WE'RE *NOT* WORRIED ABOUT
   The ruled-out items. Naming what's healthy is a credibility multiplier.

5. THE NEXT STEP
   Deep diagnosis (confirm + size with real data) and/or a WhatsApp conversation.
```

The visitor should be able to read **only the headline + the Now findings** and already know what
to do — depth is available below for those who want it (progressive disclosure, again).

---

## 4. What the report must NOT be

- **Not a dashboard.** No wall of charts and metrics to interpret. Every number serves a decision;
  nothing is shown "for completeness."
- **Not a PDF.** It's a living, interactive decision surface — findings expand, evidence is
  inspectable, the deep-diagnosis path is one tap. (A clean exportable version can exist, but the
  *experience* is not a document download.)
- **Not overload.** 3–5 prioritised findings, not 25 flags. The Brain's GUARD and scoring (20)
  already filtered to what matters; the report reflects that discipline.
- **Not fabricated certainty.** Honest confidence and explicit missing-data, always. A
  Medium-confidence finding is framed as a smart, cheap thing to confirm — not overstated.

---

## 5. Honesty as the report's signature

The report's credibility comes from what generic AI audits never do:
- It says **how sure** it is, in plain language, per finding.
- It says **what it couldn't assess** and exactly what data would change that.
- It **separates revenue from profit** in impact (heuristic H1) — many "growth" findings are
  margin warnings.
- It shows **ruled-out** items.
- It frames Medium-confidence findings as *"do this cheap thing; it helps and it confirms the
  diagnosis"* — turning uncertainty into a smart next move (Brain heuristic H10).

This honesty is not a hedge; for a sceptical operator it is the most persuasive thing on the page.

---

## 6. From preview to full report (the conversion mechanics)

- The **preview** delivers genuine, complete-feeling value (real findings, real reasoning).
- The **full report / deep diagnosis** adds: more findings, higher confidence, sized impact, and
  the executable plan — unlocked by sharing data (capture) and/or a conversation.
- Capture is asked **after** the preview's value lands (`Lead_Capture_Experience.md`), framed as
  "where do we send it / let's pressure-test these against your real numbers."

The preview is not a teaser that withholds; it's a real diagnosis whose *natural* next step is to
go deeper — which is exactly the fast-judgement → deep-diagnosis bridge of the Brain (20, §6).

---

## 7. Mobile report

- Leads with the **headline diagnosis** and the single top finding, full-width and legible.
- Findings are **expandable cards** (collapsed to finding + confidence + priority; tap for
  evidence/impact/action).
- The deep-diagnosis and WhatsApp actions are sticky.
- No dense tables; impact shown as a clear range, not a chart grid.

---

## 8. User-State Transition (this surface)

| Lens | Report Preview |
|---|---|
| **State entering** | 4 Evidence — "prove it" |
| **State leaving** | 5 Trust → 6 Action — "they understand my business; here's what to do first" |
| **Friction** | "AI nonsense / overwhelm / now what" → removed by honest confidence, missing-data, 3–5 prioritised findings, one clear first action |
| **Trust gained** | the core trust payload — system narrative, ruled-out items, profit≠revenue, evidence per finding |
| **Intelligence revealed** | ranked findings with evidence/confidence/impact/priority/action + "Growmerce can execute"; withholds full sizing/plan for deep diagnosis |
| **Next action** | capture + go deeper / WhatsApp |

---

## 9. The success test

A busy operator reads the report preview on their phone and thinks: *"They found the real
problem, they're honest about what they don't know yet, they told me what's costing me money and
what to do first — and they can actually run the fix. I should talk to them."* If the report
produces that, it has done its job as the Brain explaining itself.
