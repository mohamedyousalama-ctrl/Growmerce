# 04 — Website UX

> The user-facing interface of the Growmerce Intelligence Brain. This workstream defines **how
> the Growmerce website converts intelligence into experience** — how it turns attention, pain,
> and curiosity into diagnostics, conversations, and Growth Operations opportunities.

It is not a website build. It contains **no frontend code, no visual design, no brand system.**
It defines the *experience strategy* that a brand identity (03, next) and an eventual build will
express.

---

## The one idea

> The Growmerce website is not a brochure for an agency. It is the **Commerce Intelligence
> Experience** — the public, fast-judgement mode of the Intelligence Brain (20), rendered as
> something a busy operator can feel in three seconds.

A visitor should leave thinking: *"This is not an agency. This is a commerce intelligence
operating system — and it understands my online sales problems better than I do."*

That feeling is produced not by claiming intelligence but by **showing it quickly** — naming the
visitor's exact sales/profit leak as a recognisable pattern (19), with evidence and honest
confidence (20), before asking for anything.

---

## What Growmerce is (and what the site must never look like)

Growmerce (جرومرس) is an **Online Sales Growth Operations** company. It helps businesses grow
online sales across ecommerce stores, marketplaces, delivery apps, social commerce, WhatsApp,
and company websites.

The **paid service is Growth Operations.** Free tools, diagnostics, reports, and intelligence
assets are **lead magnets and proof of expertise** — never the product.

The site must never read as: a marketing agency, a content company, a generic SaaS dashboard, a
tools company, or a simple AI audit site. Every UX decision in this workstream is pressure-tested
against that list.

Website language: **Arabic Fusha** (practical business Arabic, RTL-first). See
`Arabic_Content_UX.md`.

## How this builds on 19 and 20 (conceptual dependencies)

- **20 Growmerce Intelligence Brain** — the website *is* the Brain's public surface. The
  homepage runs fast-judgement mode; the diagnostic runs the cognition pipeline; the report is
  the Brain explaining itself; confidence/evidence/honesty come straight from the Trust layer.
- **19 Commerce Intelligence Patterns** — the patterns are the website's recognition engine.
  Every "that's exactly my business" moment is a pattern rendered as UX (per 19's
  `Website_Intelligence_Usage.md`).
- **01–18** — approved strategic context, referenced as conceptual dependencies only; **not
  reconstructed**. The diagnostic (06), input layer (13), agents (07), packages (11), delivery
  (12), lead gen (08/09), and TikTok (12) are named where the UX connects to them.

> Where 19 gave the vocabulary and 20 gave the mind, **04 gives the face** — the surface through
> which the world experiences Growmerce's intelligence and decides to talk to it.

---

## Quality bar

This documentation succeeds if it makes one thing obvious: the website is the **user-facing
interface of an intelligence operating system**, not a marketing site. Every page earns trust by
demonstrating commercial judgement, names the visitor's real problem before asking for anything,
and makes the next step (diagnose / use a tool / message on WhatsApp) feel like the obvious,
low-risk thing to do next.
