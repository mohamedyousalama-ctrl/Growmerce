# Decisions — Website UX

Locked decisions for this workstream. Build on these; do not re-litigate. Changes require a new
decision entry.

---

### D-04.1 — The website is a Commerce Intelligence Experience, not a brochure
The site is the **user-facing interface of the Growmerce Intelligence Brain (20)**. It proves
intelligence by demonstrating it, not by claiming it. Target feeling: *"This is not an agency.
This is a commerce intelligence operating system."*

### D-04.2 — User states are primary; pages are secondary
The workstream is organised around **seven user states / cognitive transitions** — Curiosity →
Recognition → Diagnosis → Evidence → Trust → Action → Commitment (`User_State_Journey.md`). Pages
are merely containers that earn state transitions. Every surface document carries a **User-State
Transition block** (state entering / leaving · friction · trust gained · intelligence revealed ·
next action).

### D-04.3 — Retention is a primary goal, not an afterthought
The site converts visitors into **recurring intelligence users**, not just leads. Target outcome:
*"I want to come back,"* not *"I downloaded a report."* Governed by
`Intelligence_Retention_and_Return_Loops.md` (saved profiles, opportunity tracking, benchmarks,
follow-ups, history, future account). Retention is honest only — no dark patterns.

### D-04.4 — Show intelligence before asking for anything
Value precedes capture, always. A real, specific insight about the visitor's own business appears
before any email/WhatsApp field. (Strategy P1.)

### D-04.5 — Recognition over explanation; patterns, not categories
The site makes the visitor recognise themselves via named patterns (19) in their own felt words
("your discount increases orders but destroys contribution margin"), never via generic service
descriptions or categories.

### D-04.6 — Evidence-aware and honestly confident
Every insight carries *how we'd know* and *how sure we are*, in the Brain's four-band language
(20). "We strongly suspect, here's how we'd confirm" over false certainty. Honesty is a
conversion asset, and the site never fabricates numbers or problems.

### D-04.7 — Commerce-intelligence IA, not agency navigation
No Home/Services/About/Contact. Navigation is organised by the operator's problems and outcomes
(Diagnose Sales Leaks, Tools by outcome, Intelligence Patterns, Markets, Growth Operations,
Reports, Talk to Growmerce). No "About"; trust is earned by demonstrated intelligence. WhatsApp
and "Diagnose" are persistent.

### D-04.8 — Structured input feels like a command experience, not a form
The input is Apple Spotlight / Raycast — suggestions, accept/reject/edit/add, editable chips,
progressive disclosure, honest file/URL states, visible confidence — never a Typeform/Google
Forms clone. This is the primary tactile proof of intelligence.

### D-04.9 — The report is an executive decision document
Not a dashboard, not a PDF, not data overload. 3–5 prioritised findings, each with finding,
evidence, confidence, missing data, impact (revenue *and* profit, separated), effort, priority,
recommended action, and "what Growmerce can execute." Decision-first, honest.

### D-04.10 — Tools are organised by outcome and are proof of expertise, not the product
Outcome hubs (Find Revenue Leaks, Find Profit Leaks, Improve Marketplace Performance…), each tool
framed by the pattern it detects, with an example insight shown before use. The paid service is
**Online Sales Growth Operations**; free tools/diagnostics/reports are lead magnets that make
Growth Operations the obvious next step.

### D-04.11 — WhatsApp is the primary conversation channel
Persistent and context-aware. The consultation is framed as a working session with a commerce
operator ("we'll pressure-test on your real numbers"), never a vague sales call. It is the bridge
from fast-judgement (website) to deep diagnosis (engagement).

### D-04.12 — Mobile- and TikTok-first
The default visitor is on a phone from a 30-second video. The mobile flow honours the video's hook
exactly, delivers recognition + a real finding in a few taps, and keeps one-tap WhatsApp always
present. Friction is the enemy; value is front-loaded.

### D-04.13 — Arabic Fusha, composed natively, RTL-first
Practical operator Fusha — clear, specific, concrete. Not over-formal, not marketing cliché, not
English-translated. The Arabic *is* the product's voice and a primary trust signal
(`Arabic_Content_UX.md`).

### D-04.14 — Trust is built by demonstration, not testimonials
The nine proofs (pattern recognition, evidence-awareness, confidence, channel understanding,
operational language, diagnostic logic, example findings, before/after thinking, Growth Ops
clarity) lead. Honest confidence, ruled-out findings, and profit≠revenue are the sharpest trust
tools. No dark patterns, ever — a single manipulative moment drops the visitor to scepticism.

### D-04.15 — Edge cases degrade gracefully and never fabricate
No data, only a URL, messy/sensitive files, low confidence, no pattern found — each is handled
with honesty, calibrated abstention, and a forward path, protecting the user's state. Refusing to
invent a problem builds more trust than a manufactured finding (GUARD, 20).

### D-04.16 — Every surface maps to the Brain
`Website_to_Intelligence_Brain_Mapping.md` is the contract: each surface maps to a user state,
the intelligence it uses (20), the pattern layer (19), the action it drives, and the business
outcome. A surface that can't be mapped doesn't belong on the site.

### D-04.17 — Scope boundaries
This workstream defines **experience strategy only**: no frontend code, no visual design, no
brand system, no positioning change. Growmerce remains an Online Sales Growth Operations company.
01–18 are conceptual dependencies, referenced not reconstructed; 19 and 20 are the strongest
on-disk inputs. Recommended next: **03-brand-identity** (visual system to express this experience).
