# Market Page Experience

> Market pages are where a visitor recognises *their kind of business* and its specific leaks.
> They are the site's primary SEO and self-recognition surface — a restaurant operator, a
> marketplace seller, or a fashion brand should land and immediately think *"this is built for
> me, and they already know my problems."*

Market pages render the playbooks (18, conceptual context) and the patterns (19) through the
Brain's reasoning (20), localised to a sector.

---

## 1. The principle: recognition by sector

The homepage recognises the visitor by *pattern*. Market pages recognise them by *context* —
their industry and channel reality — and then connect that context to the relevant patterns,
tools, diagnostic modules, and Growth Operations package. Context-recognition + pattern-
recognition together produce the strongest "this is exactly my business" effect.

> Patterns are context-free (19); market pages re-attach them to a context so a sector operator
> sees their world precisely. The page says "in restaurants on delivery apps, this is what
> usually leaks" — pattern, localised.

## 3. Anatomy of a market page

Each page contains exactly what the brief requires, sequenced recognition-first:

```
1. SECTOR RECOGNITION (hero)
   "If you sell <market> online, here's where sales usually leak."
   One or two instantly-recognisable sector patterns.

2. COMMON SALES (REVENUE) LEAKS        ← patterns, localised
3. COMMON PROFIT LEAKS                  ← profit patterns, localised (H1 separation)
4. CHANNEL RISKS                        ← concentration/dependency for this sector
5. COMPETITOR RISKS                     ← competitive patterns common here
6. RELEVANT TOOLS                       ← outcome tools for this market
7. RELEVANT DIAGNOSTIC MODULES          ← the diagnostic, pre-scoped to this sector
8. RELEVANT GROWMERCE SERVICE PACKAGE   ← the Growth Ops package (11) for this market
9. CTA                                  ← diagnose (pre-loaded) / WhatsApp
```

Sections 2–5 are **Recognition Cards** (`Pattern_Recognition_Experience.md`) localised to the
sector — cold recognition that earns the diagnostic click. Section 8 keeps the **paid service
honest and visible** (Strategy P9). Section 9 routes into the diagnostic already pre-loaded with
the sector context, so the visitor enters warm.

## 4. How market pages connect the whole system

A market page is a hub that cross-links the entire intelligence web for one sector:

```
MARKET PAGE (e.g. Marketplace Sellers)
   ├─► patterns common here  → individual pattern pages (recognition + diagnostic)
   ├─► tools for this market  → outcome tool pages
   ├─► diagnostic, pre-scoped → the guided flow with sector context loaded
   ├─► Growth Ops package      → the paid service for this sector
   └─► WhatsApp                 → conversation, already context-aware
```

This mirrors the Brain's context gate (heuristic H6, 20): the same intelligence, interpreted
through the sector's reality.

## 5. The SEO role (search curiosity → recognition)

Market pages (with pattern pages) are the **search surface**. They target the phrases sector
operators actually search:
- "why are my Talabat orders dropping"
- "marketplace sales went down"
- "my online store gets traffic but no sales"
- "discount not making profit"

The page meets that query with **recognition, not a blog post** — naming the likely leak and
routing into the diagnostic. Search curiosity becomes a diagnostic start. (IA URLs:
`/markets/<market>`.)

---

## 6. Honesty & specificity rules

- Market recognitions are **specific to the sector's real mechanics**, not generic "grow your
  business" copy — a delivery-app page talks menus, availability, and occasion; a marketplace
  page talks reviews, buy-box, and rank.
- They are framed as **common patterns to test**, not assertions about this exact visitor (cold
  recognition honesty, per `Pattern_Recognition_Experience.md`).
- Profit is separated from revenue on every market page (H1).
- The relevant Growth Ops package is described in **outcomes and operations**, not as a feature
  list or a price tag (pricing is a conversation; the service is operations).

## 7. Mobile market page

- Hero sector-recognition + one leak above the fold.
- Leaks/risks as swipeable Recognition Cards.
- Tools/diagnostic/package as compact, tappable rows.
- Sticky "Diagnose my <market>" + WhatsApp.

## 8. User-State Transition (this surface)

| Lens | Market Page |
|---|---|
| **State entering** | 1 Curiosity / 2 Recognition — often from a sector search query |
| **State leaving** | 2 Recognition (by sector) → 3 Diagnosis |
| **Friction** | "generic advice, not for my sector" → removed by sector-specific mechanics (menus, reviews, response flow) |
| **Trust gained** | context-recognition: "this is built for *my* kind of business" |
| **Intelligence revealed** | the sector's common revenue/profit leaks, channel/competitor risks; withholds personalised proof for the diagnostic |
| **Next action** | sector-scoped diagnostic / relevant tool / WhatsApp |
