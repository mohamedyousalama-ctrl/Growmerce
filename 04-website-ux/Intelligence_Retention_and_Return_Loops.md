# Intelligence Retention & Return Loops

> A foundational document. The website's job is not finished when a visitor becomes a lead. The
> deeper goal is to turn visitors into **recurring intelligence users** — people who come back
> not because they were re-marketed to, but because Growmerce's intelligence keeps getting more
> useful to them. The target outcome is *"I want to come back,"* not *"I downloaded a report."*

This reframes the whole site: from a conversion funnel into the front of an **intelligence
platform.** It sits alongside `User_State_Journey.md` as a primary spine document, and it
influences diagnostics, tools, reports, profiles, AI agents, lead generation, and future SaaS.

---

## 1. Why users return

A one-time report is consumed and forgotten. Users return when the intelligence becomes a
**living relationship with their own business data** that compounds in value. The return drivers:

| Return driver | Why it pulls them back |
|---|---|
| **Their data lives here** | A saved structured commerce profile they don't want to rebuild |
| **The picture changes** | Sales, rank, margin, competitors move — the intelligence updates |
| **Unfinished opportunity** | Findings they haven't acted on yet, tracked and waiting |
| **New patterns surface** | The Brain learns; new recognitions appear for their business |
| **Peer benchmarking** | "How do I compare?" is an evergreen, recurring question |
| **Progress feedback** | "Did the fix work?" — they return to see movement |
| **Occasion timing** | Seasonal/occasion intelligence is inherently recurring |

The unifying principle: **the second visit must be more valuable than the first, and the tenth
more valuable than the second** — because by then Growmerce knows their business.

---

## 2. Intelligence addiction loops (healthy, honest)

A return loop is a cycle where using the intelligence creates a reason to use it again. These are
built on genuine value, never dark patterns (which would destroy the trust the journey earned).

```
        ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
        ▼                                                │
  USE intelligence  →  GET a useful, specific insight    │
        │                                                │
        ▼                                                │
  ACT (or intend to)  →  the picture/opportunity changes  │
        │                                                │
        ▼                                                │
  RETURN to see what changed / what's next  ──────────────┘
```

Core loops:
1. **Opportunity loop** — findings tracked → "you haven't acted on these 3" → return to progress them.
2. **Change loop** — their metrics/competitors move → "something shifted in your business" → return.
3. **Discovery loop** — the Brain surfaces a new pattern for them → "we noticed something new" → return.
4. **Benchmark loop** — peer comparison updates → "your position vs peers changed" → return.
5. **Progress loop** — a fix's success measure moves → "here's the impact of what you did" → return.

Each loop is grounded in real intelligence change, honest, and easy to leave — the pull is value,
not manipulation. (This mirrors the Brain's Learning Loop, 20: as Growmerce learns, the user's
intelligence improves, giving them a real reason to return.)

---

## 3. Progressive intelligence disclosure (across visits)

Just as intelligence is revealed progressively *within* the journey (`User_State_Journey.md`), it
is revealed progressively *across visits*. The platform always has a worthwhile "next thing" to
show a returning user:

| Visit | What's newly unlocked |
|---|---|
| Visit 1 | Recognition + fast-judgement findings |
| Visit 2 | Deeper findings as more data is added; first opportunity tracking |
| Visit 3+ | Benchmarks, trends, new patterns, progress on prior fixes |
| Ongoing | A compounding picture of their business that no competitor's report can match |

This is the antidote to "downloaded a report and left": there is always more intelligence to
return for, and it's gated by *their accumulating data*, not by a paywall trick.

## 4. The retention mechanisms (10 required)

### 1. Why users return
Covered in §1 — a saved, living, compounding intelligence relationship.

### 2. Intelligence addiction loops
Covered in §2 — opportunity / change / discovery / benchmark / progress loops, honest by design.

### 3. Progressive intelligence disclosure
Covered in §3 — always a worthwhile next reveal, gated by accumulating data.

### 4. Saved structured commerce profiles
The visitor's structured input (`Structured_Input_Experience.md`, 13) is **saved** as a reusable
commerce profile — channels, SKUs, competitors, pricing, occasions. They never rebuild it; every
return starts from a richer baseline. This is the gravitational center of retention: the profile
is *theirs* and lives here.

### 5. Benchmarking against peers
Evergreen recurring value: "how do you compare to similar businesses on the signals that matter?"
Benchmarks update as the peer set and their own data move — an inherently return-worthy question,
delivered honestly (ranges, confidence, anonymised peers).

### 6. Opportunity tracking
Findings become a **tracked list** with status (open / in progress / done) and impact. The user
returns to advance opportunities and see what acting (or not acting) changed. Turns a static
report into a living backlog.

### 7. Pattern discovery journeys
As the Brain learns and their data grows, **new patterns** surface over time — guided "we noticed
something new in your business" journeys. The intelligence is never "finished," so there's always
a reason to come back.

### 8. Follow-up reports
Periodic, change-triggered reports ("here's what moved this month / before the occasion") — not
spammy schedules, but **intelligence-triggered** updates tied to real change in their data or
calendar. Each is a return invitation grounded in something real.

### 9. Intelligence history
A timeline of their diagnoses, findings, actions, and outcomes — the story of their business
through Growmerce's eyes. History creates switching cost and a sense of an ongoing relationship,
and lets the user see progress over time.

### 10. Future account experience
The natural endpoint: a lightweight **account** that holds the saved profile, opportunity tracker,
benchmarks, history, and follow-ups — the bridge from "website" to "intelligence platform" and the
seed of a future SaaS product. Scoped here as a direction, not a build.

## 5. First / Return / Long-term value per surface

Per the brief, every major surface is defined across three time horizons:

| Surface | First-visit value | Return-visit value | Long-term value |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Homepage** | recognition of a leak | "what's new for my business" entry | the dashboard to their living intelligence |
| **Diagnostic** | a real first diagnosis | re-run with more data → higher confidence | a tracked diagnostic history / trend |
| **Structured input** | fast, intelligent capture | profile pre-loaded; just update what changed | a rich, compounding commerce profile |
| **Tools** | one focused insight | re-check a metric over time | ongoing monitoring of their outcomes |
| **Report preview** | prioritised findings | progress on prior findings + new ones | the running record of decisions + impact |
| **Market pages** | sector self-recognition | updated sector/occasion intelligence | staying ahead of their market over time |
| **Lead capture / WhatsApp** | warm first conversation | continuity ("last time we found…") | an ongoing operator relationship |
| **Benchmarks** (new) | first peer comparison | how their position moved | sustained competitive awareness |
| **Opportunity tracker** (new) | the prioritised plan | what's done / what's next | a living growth backlog |

Designing for all three horizons is what turns the site from a brochure into a platform.

## 6. Honest retention only (the guardrail)

Retention must never compromise the trust the journey is built on:
- **No dark patterns, fake notifications, or manufactured urgency** — the same audience that
  distrusts hype will distrust manipulative re-engagement.
- **Every return invitation is grounded in real intelligence change** (their data moved, an
  occasion approaches, a finding is unactioned, a new pattern emerged) — never "we miss you" spam.
- **Easy to leave.** Clear control over data, follow-ups, and account. Trust is the platform's
  moat; retention tactics that erode it are net-negative.
- **Privacy-first.** Saved profiles and history follow the data-use honesty of
  `Lead_Capture_Experience.md` and `UX_Edge_Cases.md`.

## 8. User-State Transition (this surface)

| Lens | Retention & Return Loops |
|---|---|
| **State entering** | a *returning* user, somewhere past Trust (5) — they already believe |
| **State leaving** | re-engaged: a new Action (6) or Commitment (7), now on accumulated context |
| **Friction** | "I already got my report / nothing new here" → removed by progressive disclosure + real change |
| **Trust gained** | the relationship deepens; Growmerce *remembers and tracks* their business |
| **Intelligence revealed** | what changed, what's new, how they compare, what's next — gated by their accumulating data |
| **Next action** | progress an opportunity, add data, view benchmark, or talk again |
