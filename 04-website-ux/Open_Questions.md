# Open Questions — Website UX

Unresolved questions for the Website UX. Surfaced deliberately; none block the strategy, all
inform brand (03), build, and the AI agents.

---

## User states & journey

1. **State measurement.** How do we instrument the seven user states (Curiosity → Commitment) in
   a real build so we can see where transitions fail? What's the proxy signal for each state?
2. **Multi-entry state-setting.** Visitors enter at different states (TikTok = Curiosity; a
   returning user = past Trust). How does each surface detect entering-state and adapt the reveal?
3. **State regression recovery.** When a visitor drops back a state (e.g. hits friction at
   Diagnosis), what's the recovery pattern that re-advances them without starting over?

## Diagnostic & input

4. **Fast-mode credibility floor.** How much can the diagnostic conclude from a URL + a few
   answers before honesty requires "we need more data"? Where exactly is the line that keeps it
   credible, not thin?
5. **Suggestion accuracy.** The Spotlight-style suggestions (inferred competitors/categories) are
   a core "wow." What accuracy is required before they build trust rather than erode it, and how
   do we handle confident-but-wrong suggestions gracefully?
6. **Input depth vs. friction.** How much structured input is the sweet spot before form-fatigue
   outweighs confidence gains, per cognition mode (quick vs. deep)?

## Trust & honesty

7. **Honest confidence vs. conversion.** We assert honest confidence converts better with
   sceptics. Needs validation — is there a segment for whom hedged language reduces conversion,
   and how do we phrase Medium confidence to maximise both trust and action?
8. **Ruled-out findings.** How prominently should "what's *not* wrong" appear without diluting the
   urgency of what *is* wrong?

## Retention & return

9. **Account threshold.** At what point does a user get a saved profile / account — immediately,
   after first diagnosis, or after capture? Too early adds friction; too late loses the data.
10. **Return-trigger honesty line.** Where is the line between a genuine intelligence-triggered
    follow-up and spammy re-engagement? What change-magnitude justifies a return invitation?
11. **Benchmark data.** Peer benchmarking needs a peer dataset. Where does it come from (aggregated
    diagnostic data?), and what's the consent/privacy model for contributing to it?
12. **Retention vs. paid service.** If the site becomes a recurring intelligence platform, how do
    we keep free retention value from cannibalising the paid Growth Operations engagement vs.
    feeding it? Where's the free/paid line for returning users?

## Arabic & localisation

13. **Terminology lock.** The core Arabic vocabulary (تسرّب المبيعات، الأنماط، عمليات النمو…) must
    be finalised with a native commerce writer and used consistently sitewide. Who owns it?
14. **Dialect reality.** Fusha is the standard, but operators speak dialect on WhatsApp. Do
    WhatsApp/CTA touchpoints flex toward a warmer, lightly-colloquial register while the site
    stays Fusha?
15. **Market/region scope.** Platform sets differ by market (e.g. Talabat/HungerStation/Jahez/
    KeeTa vary by country). How do market pages localise per region without fragmenting?

## Scope & handoff

16. **Brand handoff (03).** Which decisions are locked here (psychology, states, flows, reveal)
    vs. open for brand to shape (visual metaphors for the command center, ecosystem map,
    recognition cards)?
17. **SaaS boundary.** The retention/account stack seeds a future SaaS product. When does it stop
    being "the website" and become a product workstream of its own?
