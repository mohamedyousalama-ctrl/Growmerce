# Homepage Experience

> The homepage is the Commerce Command Center. In three seconds it must establish that Growmerce
> increases online sales, understands every commerce channel, is more intelligent and
> operational than an agency, and that the visitor can start by diagnosing their own sales
> leaks. It is the Intelligence Brain's first handshake with the market.

---

## 1. The 3-second job

Before any scroll, the visitor must absorb four things:

1. **Growmerce increases online sales.** (the outcome)
2. **It understands marketplaces, ecommerce, delivery apps, social commerce, and WhatsApp.** (the
   breadth of fluency)
3. **It is more intelligent and operational than a marketing agency.** (the difference)
4. **You can start by diagnosing your sales leaks.** (the action)

These are delivered not as four sentences but as one composed moment: a precise headline, a
recognisable channel ecosystem, and a single dominant action.

---

## 2. The hero: Commerce Command Center

The hero is not a slogan over a stock photo. It is a **command-center moment** that feels like
looking at an intelligence console for online sales.

**Composition (described, not designed — visuals are 03):**
- **Headline (Arabic Fusha):** an operator-grade statement of outcome + intelligence, e.g. a
  line that says, in essence, *"We find where your online sales leak — and operate the fixes."*
  Concrete, not slogan-y (`Arabic_Content_UX.md` governs the wording).
- **Sub-line:** names the channels (marketplaces, stores, delivery apps, social, WhatsApp) so the
  visitor instantly sees their world represented.
- **Primary action:** a single, dominant **"Diagnose Sales Leaks"** entry — ideally an *active*
  entry (a command-style input inviting "paste your store/listing link" or "choose your
  channel"), so the Brain begins working immediately, not a passive button.
- **Live recognition strip:** a rotating set of real **patterns** (19) rendered as one-line
  recognitions ("your discount lifts orders but erases contribution margin"), proving fluency in
  the first glance.

The hero's emotional target: *"this looks like a system that already understands my business."*

## 3. The scroll narrative (the homepage as a guided realisation)

After the hero, the homepage is a sequence of recognition → proof → entry. Each section earns the
next scroll.

### Section A — Sales Leak Recognition
A set of recognisable leak patterns (revenue and profit) phrased in the operator's own words. The
visitor scans and thinks *"that one… that's us."* Each links into the diagnostic pre-loaded with
that pattern. This is the homepage's most important section — recognition before explanation
(Strategy P2). Examples drawn from 19:
- "Traffic reaches your page, but trust isn't strong enough to buy."
- "Your discount increases orders but destroys contribution margin."
- "Your competitor wins because the offer is clearer — not because the price is lower."
- "Your delivery-app menu hides the items that should be selling."
- "Your WhatsApp inquiries aren't becoming orders — the response flow is weak."

### Section B — The commerce ecosystem map
Instead of a logo wall, a **commerce ecosystem visualization**: the channels and platforms
(Amazon, Noon, Talabat, HungerStation, Jahez, KeeTa, Salla, Zid, TikTok Shop, Shopify,
WooCommerce, WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok) shown as an **intelligence map** — nodes Growmerce
reasons across, not badges it borrows credibility from. The message: *"we operate across your
whole commerce surface, and we understand how they interconnect."* (Decision: platforms are only
ever shown as part of an ecosystem/intelligence map — never a trust-borrowing logo wall.)

### Section C — Pattern-based proof
A deeper taste of intelligence: one or two **worked recognitions** showing finding → evidence →
confidence → recommended action (a micro `Report_Preview_Experience.md`). This *demonstrates*
the Brain rather than describing it (Strategy P1/P3). Honesty included ("we'd confirm this by…").

### Section D — Diagnostic entry (the main conversion)
A focused invitation to run the full diagnostic, framed as fast, free, and genuinely useful. Low
friction, value-forward. This is the homepage's primary destination.

### Section E — What Growth Operations actually is
A clear, honest explanation that the **paid service is Online Sales Growth Operations** — not
ads, not content, not a dashboard. Growmerce *operates the fixes*, it doesn't just point at them.
This prevents the free tools from misrepresenting the business (Strategy P9). Links to
`Growth Operations` and WhatsApp.

### Section F — Markets & tools on-ramps
Compact entries into `Markets` (recognise yourself by sector) and outcome-organised `Tools`,
for visitors who prefer to self-navigate.

### Persistent
Sticky **Diagnose** and **WhatsApp** actions throughout.

## 4. What the homepage must NOT be

- Not a generic hero + logo wall + services grid + testimonials + footer.
- Not a wall of equal CTAs — one dominant action (Diagnose), WhatsApp persistent, the rest
  secondary (Strategy P8).
- Not claims without demonstration — every claim is paired with a shown recognition or worked
  example.
- Not marketing language — operator language throughout (Strategy P4).
- Not English-structure-translated-into-Arabic — composed natively in Arabic Fusha.

---

## 5. Mobile homepage (the default)

Most visitors arrive on a phone, often from TikTok. The mobile homepage:
- Leads with **one** recognition + the diagnostic entry above the fold.
- Collapses the ecosystem map into a recognisable, swipeable channel strip.
- Keeps WhatsApp one thumb-tap away at all times.
- Defers depth (ecosystem, Growth Ops explanation) below, for those who keep scrolling.
See `Mobile_TikTok_Landing_Experience.md` for the campaign-landing variant.

## 7. User-State Transition (this surface)

| Lens | Homepage |
|---|---|
| **State entering** | 1 Curiosity — "I think something is wrong" (often arriving cold from TikTok/search) |
| **State leaving** | 2 Recognition — "I think this problem is happening to me" |
| **Friction** | "another agency / not relevant to me" → removed by 3-second operator-grade specificity + recognition strip |
| **Trust gained** | initial credibility from precision and channel fluency (the ecosystem map, not a logo wall) |
| **Intelligence revealed** | high-recognition patterns + one worked proof; withholds personalised evidence/confidence for the diagnostic |
| **Next action** | start the diagnostic (or enter on a specific recognition) |

---

## 8. The success test

A busy operator lands, and within three seconds thinks *"these people get online sales,"* within
fifteen seconds recognises at least one of their own leaks, and within a minute either starts the
diagnostic or opens WhatsApp — all without Growmerce having made a single unsupported claim. If
the homepage produces that sequence, it is doing its job as the face of the Intelligence Brain.
