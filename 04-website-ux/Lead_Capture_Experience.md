# Lead Capture Experience

> Lead capture must never feel like a gate slammed in front of value. The site gives first and
> asks second. Capture happens *after* the visitor has felt the intelligence — and it's framed
> as continuing a valuable conversation, not paying an entry toll.

---

## 1. The principle: value before capture

The single rule (Strategy P1): the visitor must receive a real, specific insight about their own
business **before** any email/WhatsApp field appears. A sceptical, busy operator will give
contact details only once they believe there's something worth continuing — and the only way to
create that belief is to demonstrate it first.

```
❌ Gate-first        Land → email wall → maybe value      → bounce
✅ Value-first       Land → recognition → preview insight → "where do we send the rest?" → capture
```

---

## 2. When to ask (the capture moments)

Capture is offered at moments of **earned momentum**, never cold:

| Moment | Why it works | What's already been given |
|---|---|---|
| After **report preview** | Peak perceived value | Real findings + reasoning |
| After a **tool insight** | Focused value delivered | A worked recognition |
| To **save / continue** a diagnostic | Loss-aversion + convenience | Partial analysis they don't want to lose |
| To **go deeper** (deep diagnosis) | Clear value exchange | Fast-mode findings + the offer of confirmation |
| Into **WhatsApp** | Lowest-friction continuation | Everything above; conversation feels natural |

The default and strongest moment is **after the report preview** — the visitor has just seen the
Brain diagnose their business and naturally wants the full version / the next step.

---

## 3. What value is shown before capture

- At least one **warm recognition** (a pattern matched to their own input).
- A **report preview** with real findings, evidence, and honest confidence.
- A clear sense of **what more they'll get** by continuing (deeper findings, sizing, the
  executable plan) — so the exchange is transparent, not a mystery box.

The visitor should always be able to answer: *"what did I get, and what will I get?"* before
deciding to share anything.

---

## 4. Minimum data, maximum clarity

- Ask for the **least** needed for the next step — usually **WhatsApp number or email**, nothing
  more. No company-size/role/budget interrogation at capture (that's what the conversation is for).
- Pre-fill/carry over anything already provided (a store URL, sector) so capture is one field,
  not a form restart.
- Every field has an obvious **why**: "where should we send your full report?" beats "enter your
  email." (Arabic Fusha wording in `Arabic_Content_UX.md`.)

---

## 5. Permission, honestly asked

- State plainly **what happens next** ("we'll send your report and, if useful, a short note on
  the highest-impact fix — no spam").
- Ask **explicit permission** to continue on WhatsApp/email; never assume or pre-tick.
- Make the value of saying yes concrete and the cost of saying no zero (the preview is theirs
  either way where possible).

This honest, low-pressure framing is itself a trust signal to an agency-wary operator.

---

## 6. Handling upload & privacy anxiety at capture

Capture often coincides with the visitor having shared a link or file, which raises privacy
worry. The UX must:
- Reassure **before** anxiety peaks: a plain-language note on how data is used and that sensitive
  details aren't required.
- Offer a **low-commitment path** (continue on WhatsApp without uploading more; get the report
  without connecting accounts).
- Never demand sensitive commercial data to release the preview.
(Privacy edge cases in `UX_Edge_Cases.md`; sensitive-data handling in `Structured_Input_Experience.md`.)

---

## 7. What lead capture must NOT do

- Block the first insight behind an email wall.
- Ask for contact details before any value is shown.
- Use long forms, fake "calculating… enter email to see results" tricks, or dark patterns.
- Demand role/company/budget qualification on the website (qualification is a conversation, 09/10).
- Pretend the report doesn't exist until they pay with their data — value is genuinely given.

---

## 8. From capture to conversation (the handoff)

Capture is not the goal; the **conversation** is. Every capture flows immediately toward
WhatsApp or a consultation (`WhatsApp_and_Consultation_CTA.md`), with the captured context
(their findings, their inputs) carried into that conversation so it starts warm: *"we saw the
discount-margin pattern in your store — want us to pressure-test it against your real numbers?"*

A captured lead with no warm next step is a wasted capture. The design always pairs capture with
an immediate, context-aware forward action.

---

## 9. Mobile capture

- Single field (WhatsApp number, ideally) with a one-tap "send to WhatsApp" that opens the
  conversation directly — collapsing capture and conversation into one motion.
- No keyboard marathons; use device contact/autofill.
- Permission note short and visible above the action.

---

## 10. User-State Transition (this surface)

| Lens | Lead Capture |
|---|---|
| **State entering** | 6 Action — "what should I do now?" (value already delivered) |
| **State leaving** | 7 Commitment — "let's continue" |
| **Friction** | gate-too-early fear, privacy/upload anxiety → removed by value-before-capture, minimal data, honest permission |
| **Trust gained** | the low-pressure, transparent ask itself signals integrity to an agency-wary operator |
| **Intelligence revealed** | what more they'll get by continuing (deeper findings, sizing, the plan); nothing fabricated |
| **Next action** | share WhatsApp/email → warm, context-aware conversation |

---

## 11. Capture → Brain mapping (summary)

| Capture element | Brain (20) | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Timing (post-value) | ACT stage, after DECIDE | warm, willing lead |
| What's shown first | fast-judgement findings + confidence | belief that continuing is worth it |
| Minimal data | — | low friction, high completion |
| Permission framing | honesty principle (Trust layer) | trust + compliance |
| Handoff to WhatsApp | route to Growth Operations (10/11/12) | qualified conversation |

Lead capture is the hinge between free proof and paid conversation — and it works precisely
because, by the time it's asked, the visitor already believes Growmerce understands their
business.
