# Offer Patterns

> How the offer is constructed, bundled, and presented. Not "weak offer" — these are the
> recurring ways the *structure* of what's being sold helps or hurts, independent of the
> product or price.

Each pattern uses the canonical template.

---

## Pattern: Selling A Product When The Customer Is Buying An Outcome

**Category:** Offer

**Description:** The offer is framed as a product (an object with features) while the customer
is actually buying an outcome (a result, a transformation, a job done). The mismatch means the
offer competes on product attributes when it could compete on the result the customer wants —
leaving it interchangeable and price-shopped instead of uniquely valuable.

**Business Symptoms:**
- "We explain everything about the product but customers still don't see why it's special."
- Competing on features/specs in a commoditised way.
- Customers comparing on attributes, not results.
- Hard to justify a premium for a 'better object.'

**Evidence Signals:**
- Offer/messaging organised around features, not outcomes.
- Buyer language centred on the result they want, not the product attributes.
- Reviews praising outcomes the offer never marketed.
- Commodity comparison on specs in the category.

**Common Root Causes:**
- Seller thinks in product; buyer thinks in outcome.
- No outcome-based offer framing.
- Features assumed to "speak for themselves."
- Missing the job-to-be-done.

**Revenue Impact:** High — outcome framing lifts conversion and supports premium pricing.

**Profit Impact:** Favourable — outcomes are harder to price-compare, protecting margin.

**Risk Level:** Medium-High — keeps the offer stuck in commodity competition.

**Confidence Factors:**
- Feature-led offer vs. outcome-led buyer language.
- Reviews citing un-marketed outcomes.
- Spec-based category comparison.

**Counter Evidence:**
- The category genuinely is bought on specs (technical/professional buyers).
- The offer already leads with outcome and the issue is elsewhere.

**Quick Wins:**
- Reframe the hero message around the outcome the customer is buying.
- Lead the offer with the result; support with features as proof.
- Mine reviews for the outcomes customers actually value and surface them.

**Strategic Fixes:**
- Rebuild the offer architecture around the job-to-be-done.
- Develop outcome-based proof (before/after, results, testimonials).
- Position against the cost of *not* achieving the outcome.

**Operational Fixes:**
- Outcome-first standard for offer/messaging design.
- Review-mining loop to keep outcomes current.

**AI Detection Opportunities:** An agent compares offer/messaging framing against buyer-outcome
language (reviews, queries) and flags feature-led offers in outcome-led markets.

**Diagnostic Usage:** Offer-Framing module. Triggered by feature-led offer + outcome-led buyer language.

**Report Usage:** "You're selling a product; your customer is buying a result." Shown with the
feature-vs-outcome language comparison.

**Service Delivery Usage:** Offer-reframe sprint: outcome architecture, proof, messaging.

**Future SaaS Usage:** Offer-framing analyser scoring outcome vs. feature orientation.

## Pattern: The Offer Has No Reason To Buy Now

**Category:** Offer

**Description:** The offer is attractive but contains nothing that makes *now* better than
*later*, so high-intent buyers defer — and deferral is where most sales die. There's no
deadline, no scarcity, no time-bound bonus, no penalty for waiting. The business converts only
the small slice of buyers who happen to be ready this instant and silently loses the rest to
"I'll come back" (they won't).

**Business Symptoms:**
- "People are interested but they keep saying they'll come back."
- High consideration, lots of deferral, low same-session conversion.
- Carts and enquiries that go cold.
- No urgency anywhere in the offer.

**Evidence Signals:**
- Long consideration windows, high return-visit-before-purchase rates, low first-session conversion.
- No time-bound or scarcity element in the offer.
- Abandoned carts/enquiries that never convert.
- "Saving for later" behaviour dominating.

**Common Root Causes:**
- No urgency or reason-to-act-now built into the offer.
- Fear that urgency feels pushy (so none is used at all).
- Evergreen offer with no momentum.
- Relying on intent alone to overcome inertia.

**Revenue Impact:** High — deferral is the largest silent leak for considered purchases.

**Profit Impact:** Neutral-to-favourable — honest urgency converts existing intent without discounting.

**Risk Level:** Medium-High — deferred buyers are mostly lost buyers.

**Confidence Factors:**
- Measurable deferral/return-before-buy behaviour.
- Absence of any reason-to-act-now.
- Cold carts/enquiries.

**Counter Evidence:**
- The purchase genuinely requires long consideration and false urgency would backfire.
- Urgency already exists and conversion lag is from another cause.

**Quick Wins:**
- Add an honest reason to act now (time-bound bonus, limited stock, enrolment window).
- Put a deadline on the current offer.
- Add a now-vs-later value contrast at the decision point.

**Strategic Fixes:**
- Design reason-to-act-now into the offer structure (launches, seasons, bonuses).
- Build a calendar of legitimate urgency moments.
- Use scarcity/exclusivity authentically where it exists.

**Operational Fixes:**
- Offer-design standard that requires a reason-to-buy-now.
- Deferral-rate monitoring.

**AI Detection Opportunities:** An agent measures deferral/return-before-buy behaviour and
flags offers lacking any reason-to-act-now, recommending honest urgency mechanisms.

**Diagnostic Usage:** Offer-Momentum module. Triggered by high deferral + absent urgency.

**Report Usage:** "Your offer gives no reason to buy today, so most buyers don't." Shown with
the deferral/return-before-buy chart.

**Service Delivery Usage:** Offer-momentum sprint: urgency design, deadline structure, bonus calendar.

**Future SaaS Usage:** Offer-momentum analyser detecting missing reason-to-act-now.

## Pattern: Bundling By Convenience Instead Of By Logic Of Use

**Category:** Offer

**Description:** Bundles exist but are assembled by what's easy to group (or what's slow-moving
and needs clearing) rather than by how the customer actually uses the products together. The
bundle doesn't match a real use-occasion, so it neither lifts AOV nor solves a customer need —
it just looks like a discount on a random grouping.

**Business Symptoms:**
- "We have bundles but they don't really sell."
- Bundles built around clearing stock, not customer use.
- Low bundle attach rate.
- Customers building their own combinations the business doesn't offer.

**Evidence Signals:**
- Bundle composition not matching frequently co-purchased / co-used items.
- Low bundle attach/uptake.
- Customers manually combining items the bundles don't reflect.
- Slow-movers padded into bundles.

**Common Root Causes:**
- Bundles designed for inventory convenience, not use logic.
- No analysis of what's actually bought/used together.
- Bundles as a clearance tool, not a value tool.
- Missing the use-occasion the customer is solving for.

**Revenue Impact:** Medium-High — use-logic bundles lift AOV and solve real needs; convenience
bundles do neither.

**Profit Impact:** Favourable when bundles add value (premium attach) rather than discount slow stock.

**Risk Level:** Low-Medium — missed upside more than damage.

**Confidence Factors:**
- Co-purchase/co-use data contradicting bundle composition.
- Low attach on existing bundles.
- Customer self-combining behaviour.

**Counter Evidence:**
- Existing bundles already reflect real use and perform.
- The category doesn't lend itself to bundling.

**Quick Wins:**
- Rebuild the top bundle around the most frequently co-purchased items.
- Create a bundle that matches a real use-occasion the customer solves for.
- Drop slow-mover-padded bundles that aren't selling.

**Strategic Fixes:**
- Design bundles from co-purchase/co-use analysis and use-occasions.
- Build "solution" bundles around the customer's job, not the inventory.
- Use bundles to add value and lift AOV, not to clear stock.

**Operational Fixes:**
- Co-purchase analysis feeding bundle design.
- Bundle attach-rate monitoring.

**AI Detection Opportunities:** An agent mines co-purchase/co-use data and recommends
use-logic bundles, flagging convenience bundles that underperform.

**Diagnostic Usage:** Bundle-Logic module. Triggered by composition-vs-co-purchase mismatch +
low attach.

**Report Usage:** "Your bundles are grouped for your warehouse, not your customer." Shown
with co-purchase data vs. current bundle composition.

**Service Delivery Usage:** Bundle-redesign sprint based on use logic.

**Future SaaS Usage:** Bundle-recommendation engine from co-purchase/co-use patterns.
