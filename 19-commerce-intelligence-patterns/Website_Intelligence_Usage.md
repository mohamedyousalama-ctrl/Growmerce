# Website Intelligence Usage

> How patterns power the website experience. The single goal of the website is to make a
> visitor say: **"That is exactly what is happening in my business."** Patterns are the only
> asset capable of producing that moment at scale, because they describe the visitor's
> reality more precisely than they could describe it themselves.

This document bridges the Pattern Engine (19) to the upcoming **Website UX (04)** workstream —
it is the brief for how patterns should drive the site.

---

## 1. The website's job

A generic agency website says *"we grow your ecommerce business."* A pattern-powered website
says *"you're probably winning impressions but losing the click, paying for visibility you
can't convert — here's how we'd know, and how we'd fix it."* The second one stops the scroll.

The website's job is **recognition → trust → action**:
1. **Recognition** — the visitor sees their exact situation named.
2. **Trust** — the precision proves Growmerce understands commerce, not just marketing.
3. **Action** — a low-friction next step (self-diagnosis, audit) that captures the lead.

Patterns drive all three.

---

## 2. How each pattern field becomes website content

| Pattern field | Website use |
|---|---|
| **Pattern Name** | Headline / card title — the recognisable hook |
| **Business Symptoms** | "Does this sound like you?" copy — the recognition trigger (visitor's own words) |
| **Description** | The "here's what's actually happening" explainer |
| **Revenue/Profit Impact** | "What it's costing you" — the stakes |
| **Evidence Signals** | "How we'd know" — proof Growmerce is rigorous, not guessing |
| **Quick Wins** | "What you could do this week" — value given freely, builds trust |
| **Confidence/Counter Evidence** | The honesty that differentiates from hype merchants |

The symptoms field is the most valuable: it is written in the operator's own felt language, so
it produces the involuntary *"that's me"* reaction.

## 3. Website surfaces powered by patterns

### Self-diagnosis tool (the flagship)
An interactive "Which patterns are costing you the most?" experience. The visitor answers a
short set of questions (Tier 1 / light Tier 2 evidence), and the site returns their top 2–3
patterns at Possible/Probable confidence — naming the problem, sizing the impact, teasing the
fix, and capturing the lead for a deeper audit. This is the Commerce Diagnostic (06) rendered
as a website lead magnet, powered entirely by the pattern library and the trust-stack models.

### Pattern library / "symptoms" pages
SEO-rich pages, one per pattern, structured around the recognition copy. A founder searching
their exact problem ("my bestsellers keep selling out while my cash is stuck") lands on the
matching pattern page — instant recognition, instant credibility, clear next step.

### Homepage / positioning
The hero and proof sections lead with a rotating set of the highest-recognition patterns, not
generic claims. "We find the patterns costing you money — and fix them."

### Case framing
Results are framed as patterns resolved ("we found *Returns Are A Silent Tax Nobody Owns* and
cut return cost by X"), making outcomes concrete and repeatable-feeling.

## 4. The recognition mechanics

The "that's exactly my business" moment fires when three things align:
1. **Specificity** — the pattern is precise, not a category ("Review Density Gap," not "low
   reviews"). Precision signals understanding.
2. **Felt language** — the symptoms are phrased the way the owner actually thinks ("whenever
   we cut ad spend, sales basically stop").
3. **The unspoken made explicit** — the pattern names something the owner half-knew but
   couldn't articulate. Articulation feels like insight.

This is why the **CRITICAL RULE** (patterns, not categories) is a website rule as much as a
diagnostic rule. Categories produce indifference; patterns produce recognition.

---

## 5. From recognition to lead to engagement

```
Visitor recognises a pattern (symptoms copy)
   → engages the self-diagnosis (sees top patterns + impact)
   → captures lead for deeper audit (more evidence, higher confidence)
   → standard/deep diagnostic (paid)
   → delivery (fix playbooks)
```

The website is the **top of the same pattern funnel** that runs all the way through to
delivery — the visitor's first "that's me" and the delivered fix are the same pattern at
different evidence depths. Nothing is bolted on; it's one system rendered at different stages.

---

## 6. Honesty as a conversion asset

Counterintuitively, the Confidence Model's honesty *increases* conversion. A site that says
*"based on these few answers we strongly suspect X — a full audit would confirm it"* outsells
one that overclaims, because operators are sceptical of agencies that promise certainty they
can't have. Honest confidence framing is a differentiator, not a hedge.

---

## 7. Brief for Website UX (04)

When the Website UX workstream begins, this is its core input:
- Build the **self-diagnosis tool** as the flagship interactive surface.
- Build **one recognition page per pattern**, structured on the symptoms/description/impact/
  quick-win fields, optimised for the search terms operators actually use.
- Lead positioning with **specific patterns**, never generic growth claims.
- Carry the **honesty/confidence framing** through every claim.
- Treat the website as the **first evidence-depth tier** of the one pattern funnel, capturing
  leads for deeper diagnostics.

The patterns are ready. The website is how the world meets them.
