# Backlog — Commerce Knowledge System

Knowledge-building and knowledge-engineering work. Candidates, not commitments. Scoped to the
knowledge architecture (not UI, brand, or product vision).

---

## Domain development (depth)

The five domains developed in depth (`04`–`08`) are worked examples of the structure. The remaining
domains (02) are to be developed to the same depth:
- **Delivery Knowledge** — menu-as-storefront, availability, timing, delivery economics.
- **Ecommerce Knowledge** — store funnels, checkout, on-site trust, catalogue, lifecycle.
- **Retail Knowledge** — assortment, merchandising, inventory, omni-channel.
- **Profitability Knowledge** — fully-loaded contribution, unit economics, cost structure, cash.
- **Growth Knowledge** — constraints, leverage, compounding vs. rented growth, sequencing.
- **Conversion Knowledge** — the intent→purchase path, friction, objection, commitment moment.
- **Retention Knowledge** — repeat cadence, churn timing, loyalty vs. lock-in, LTV.
- **Channel Knowledge** — mix, attribution, concentration risk, create-vs-capture, channel-fit.
- **Operations Knowledge** — availability, fulfilment, returns, response flow, lead times.
- **Market Knowledge** — structure, maturity, saturation, differentiation space (partly in `08`).

Each to be built out with facts, principles, models, frameworks, heuristics, benchmarks, patterns,
anti-patterns, exceptions, and lessons (03).

## Unknowns & confidence

## Knowledge engineering

- **Encoding format** — store/encode knowledge objects so the Brain retrieves the right object at the
  right stage, human-readable and auditable, not buried in model weights (Open Question #12). The most
  consequential build decision for auditability.
- **Seeding plan** — initial external-knowledge import per domain, balanced against earned knowledge
  (Open Question #13).
- **Network-knowledge incorporation** — ingest aggregated cross-business knowledge as objects with
  certainty + privacy controls (Open Question #14).
