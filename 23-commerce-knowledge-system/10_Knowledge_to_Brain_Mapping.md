# 10 — Knowledge → Brain Mapping

> Where knowledge enters reasoning. The Intelligence Brain (20) is the process; the Commerce Knowledge
> System (23) is the content that process operates on. This document shows *exactly where* knowledge
> enters the Brain's cognition — because a reasoning engine with no knowledge reasons about nothing.

---

## 1. The relationship in one line

> The Brain is *how* Growmerce thinks; Knowledge is *what it thinks with.* At every stage of the
> Brain's cognition pipeline, it reaches into the Knowledge System for the object it needs —
> a benchmark to judge, a model to explain, a pattern to recognise, a playbook to recommend.

Reasoning without knowledge is empty; knowledge without reasoning is inert. The Brain and the
Knowledge System are two halves of one intelligence.

---

## 2. The general flow

```
KNOWLEDGE  →  REASONING  →  INTERPRETATION  →  DECISION
(the prior)   (the Brain     (knowledge gives    (knowledge selects
              applies it)     evidence meaning)    the right action)
```

Knowledge enters at the start (as the lens), throughout (as the interpreter), and at the end (as the
chooser). It is not a single input; it is the substance the entire process is made of.

---

## 3. Knowledge at each Brain stage (20)

The Brain's cognition pipeline is SENSE → GROUND → RECOGNISE → INTERACT → ASSESS → VALUE → DECIDE →
GUARD → ACT → LEARN. Knowledge enters at every stage:

| Brain stage (20) | Knowledge object used (03) | What knowledge does here |
|---|---|---|
| **SENSE** | Signal Taxonomy (an expression of knowledge about what's worth observing) | tells the system *what to look for* |
| **GROUND** | Benchmarks, Facts | tells the system whether a signal is normal/abnormal — gives evidence meaning |
| **RECOGNISE** | Patterns, Rules, Models | matches evidence to a recurring reality; supplies the causal account |
| **INTERACT** | Models, Principles | explains how patterns reinforce/conflict/compound/cause each other |
| **ASSESS** | Exceptions, Counter-knowledge, Benchmarks | checks whether the pattern truly applies (rules out exceptions) and calibrates confidence |
| **VALUE** | Models (impact), Heuristics, Market knowledge | estimates revenue/profit impact and severity using domain knowledge |
| **DECIDE** | Playbooks, Frameworks, Heuristics | selects and sequences the right fix for this pattern in this context |
| **GUARD** | Anti-patterns, Exceptions, Principles | catches errors against known mistakes; enforces "what would make this wrong" |
| **ACT** | Playbooks | the validated fix that execution will apply |
| **LEARN** | Lessons, Case observations | the outcome becomes new knowledge, fed back (11) |

The single most important entries are at **GROUND** (benchmarks give evidence meaning),
**RECOGNISE** (patterns + models turn meaning into a diagnosis), and **DECIDE** (playbooks turn a
diagnosis into action). These three are where "data" becomes "intelligence," and all three are
knowledge acts.

---

## 4. The four ways knowledge enters the Brain

Distilling the table, knowledge enters reasoning in four distinct roles:

### Role 1 — The lens (what to look at)
Before any evidence, knowledge defines *what matters*: which signals to seek, which patterns are worth
checking, which questions the diagnostic asks. The Brain does not look at everything; knowledge directs
attention to the vital few (the operator heuristic H2, 20). *Knowledge sets the agenda of inquiry.*

### Role 2 — The interpreter (what evidence means)
Knowledge converts verified evidence into judgement: a benchmark says whether a number is good or bad;
a model says what it implies; a rule says what follows. *This is the step that turns the Supply
Chain's verified facts into meaning* — and it is pure knowledge. Without it, the Brain would hold true
numbers it cannot understand.

### Role 3 — The reasoner's substrate (how to connect things)
When the Brain reasons about pattern interactions, causality, and impact, it uses models and
principles as the *substrate* of inference. The Brain doesn't invent that a stockout causes rank
decay; it *knows* it (a rule), and reasons with it. *Knowledge is the content of every inference.*

### Role 4 — The chooser (what to do)
At decision time, knowledge supplies the playbook and the contextual adjustments. The Brain doesn't
improvise a fix; it selects a validated one (a knowledge object) and adapts it with more knowledge
(market maturity, constraints). *Knowledge is what makes a recommendation right, not just plausible.*

---

## 5. Worked trace (knowledge entering at each step)

A business with flat sales:
1. **Lens:** knowledge says "flat sales could be a leak or thin demand — check discovery, conversion,
   availability, and demand trajectory" → directs the diagnostic (Role 1).
2. **Interpret:** evidence shows high category demand (benchmark: strong) + low impression share
   (benchmark: weak) → knowledge judges this a *discovery* gap, not a demand problem (Role 2).
3. **Recognise:** the model (visibility-velocity loop, 04) + the pattern "Demand Exists But Discovery
   Fails" → a diagnosis with a cause (Role 3).
4. **Rule out:** exception knowledge — "is this a niche where low search is normal?" → checked,
   excluded → confidence rises (Role 3, GUARD).
5. **Decide:** the discovery playbook (keyword rebuild, review velocity, listing optimisation),
   sequenced ahead of conversion work because knowledge says discovery is upstream (Role 4).
6. **Learn:** the outcome ("keyword rebuild recovered impressions fastest here") becomes a lesson that
   strengthens the playbook (11).

At every step, the *reasoning* is the Brain's; the *content* is the Knowledge System's.

---

## 6. Why separating them matters

Keeping the Brain (process) and Knowledge (content) distinct is a deliberate architecture, and it
yields three advantages:
- **The reasoning is auditable** — you can ask "which knowledge object led to this conclusion?" and
  get an answer (knowledge provenance), because knowledge is explicit, not buried in model weights.
- **The system improves specifically** — a wrong conclusion can be traced to a wrong/missing knowledge
  object and fixed *there* (add a benchmark, an exception, a lesson), rather than retraining a
  black box.
- **The same Brain gets smarter as knowledge grows** — without changing the reasoning engine, better
  knowledge produces better output. This is the mechanism of compounding (11) and the moat (15): the
  Brain is the constant; knowledge is the compounding asset it reasons with.

> A reasoning engine is a commodity; what it reasons *with* is the institution. The Brain is licensed
> intelligence-in-general; the Commerce Knowledge System is Growmerce's specific, compounding, owned
> understanding of commerce — and it is the latter that makes the former worth anything.
