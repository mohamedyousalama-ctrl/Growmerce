# Decisions — Commerce Knowledge System

Locked decisions for this workstream. Build on these; do not re-litigate.

---

### D-23.1 — The answer to "what expertise lets Growmerce recommend better" is the Commerce Knowledge System
Not AI, algorithms, models, prompts, or automation — a structured, validated, compounding body of
commercial understanding. This is a knowledge-architecture problem, approached like a serious knowledge
institution (McKinsey / Bloomberg / Palantir ontology / Amazon doctrine), not a SaaS feature.

### D-23.2 — Knowledge is the standing prior; it is distinct from patterns, signals, evidence, and recommendations
Knowledge = what is generally true about commerce (the lens). Signal = a raw observation. Evidence =
a verified signal (22). Pattern = knowledge crystallised into a recurring diagnosable reality (19).
Recommendation = knowledge applied to a confirmed pattern (20). These five are kept separate and
explicit. (`09`.)

### D-23.3 — Knowledge is organised into sixteen transferable domains
Organised by *commercial reality*, not industry or platform, so knowledge transfers and compounds.
Four families: channel/format, commercial-mechanics, demand/behaviour, competitive/operational. Each
domain has a common internal structure of knowledge objects. (`02`.)

### D-23.4 — Knowledge is expressed as fourteen typed objects
Facts, Principles, Models, Frameworks, Rules, Heuristics, Playbooks, Benchmarks, Patterns,
Anti-patterns, Assumptions, Exceptions, Lessons, Case observations — arranged on a certainty ×
generality hierarchy. Higher objects (Principles, Facts) govern lower (Assumptions, Case observations)
in conflict. Every object is typed, sourced, scoped, and versioned. (`03`.)

### D-23.5 — A commerce ontology is the shared foundation
The fundamental entities (Business, Product, Offer, Channel, Marketplace, Customer, Competitor,
Occasion, Order, Transaction, Margin, Promotion) and the intelligence entities (Signal, Evidence,
Knowledge, Pattern, Opportunity, Threat, Recommendation) and their relationships form one schema all
components refer to. A Pattern is *defined* as a recurring configuration of entities/relationships.
Revenue-vs-profit and offer-vs-product distinctions are structural, not stylistic. (`14`.)

### D-23.6 — Domain knowledge is real and transferable, not platform tactics
The marketplace, pricing/promotion, customer-behaviour, occasion/demand, and competitor/market domains
(`04`–`08`) contain genuine commercial doctrine that applies across industries and platforms. The
patterns of workstream 19 are this doctrine crystallised into diagnosable form.

### D-23.7 — Knowledge enters the Brain at every reasoning stage, in four roles
As the lens (what to look at), the interpreter (what evidence means), the reasoner's substrate (how to
connect things), and the chooser (what to do). Benchmarks at GROUND, patterns/models at RECOGNISE, and
playbooks at DECIDE are where data becomes intelligence. The Brain is the process; knowledge is the
content. Keeping them separate makes reasoning auditable and improvable. (`10`.)

### D-23.8 — Knowledge compounds through an outcome loop
New knowledge is earned primarily from execution outcomes (exclusive data), plus reasoning and
external import. It strengthens by recurring across independent contexts and being confirmed by
outcomes; it evolves (refinement, exceptions); and stale knowledge is retired (drift → deprecate →
version → replace). Contradictions are resolved by hierarchy/scope/outcome, never averaged. (`11`.)

### D-23.9 — Knowledge quality is validated, earned, reviewed, and versioned
No object has authority beyond its validation. Certainty is earned (recurrence, outcome confirmation,
explanatory power, surviving challenge) and lost (outcome contradiction, accumulating exceptions,
market shift). Outcome validation is the strongest. Every object is reviewed (promotion/drift/periodic/
contradiction/challenge), owned per domain, and versioned for auditability. Knowledge quality is
upstream of output confidence. (`12`.)

### D-23.10 — Unknowns are first-class; false certainty is structurally prevented
Gaps, assumptions, exceptions, coverage gaps, and genuine uncertainty are represented explicitly.
Assumptions can never become facts; confidence is computed from knowledge certainty + evidence, never
asserted; counter-knowledge/exceptions are checked; abstention ("we don't know yet") is a valid output.
Knowing the edge of our knowledge is itself knowledge. This is the upstream source of the brand's
calibrated confidence. (`13`.)

### D-23.11 — Institutional, compounding knowledge is the real moat
Growmerce knows more over time because it captures outcomes as validated, transferable, institutional
knowledge. Agencies' knowledge is personal and doesn't scale; consultants' doesn't compound;
dashboards have none; AI-only systems have fluency without validated/auditable understanding;
marketplace specialists don't generalise. The compounding gap widens with time and cannot be copied.
(`15`.)

### D-23.12 — Scope: knowledge only
This workstream defines *what Growmerce knows*. It does not redesign prior workstreams, reposition
Growmerce, or address recognition (19), reasoning (20), verification (22), presentation/UI/voice/
visuals (04/21/03), or selling. It builds on those as approved foundations. Tone is doctrine — no
hype, AI buzzwords, marketing, feature, or website language.
