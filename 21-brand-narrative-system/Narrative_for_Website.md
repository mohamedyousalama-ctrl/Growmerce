# Narrative for Website

> How Growmerce speaks on the website. The website is the Commerce Intelligence Experience
> (`04-website-ux`); its language must make a visitor think *"these people understand my online
> sales"* within seconds. This document governs website copy across all surfaces and states.

---

## Objective

Turn attention into recognition into trust into a diagnostic start or a conversation — using
language that **demonstrates** intelligence rather than claiming it, and never sounds like an
agency.

## Tone

Confident, calm, recognition-led, evidence-aware. Inviting but not salesy. The homepage should
feel like a sharp operator opening a conversation, not a brochure shouting a value proposition.
(Personality: analytical, direct, honest, calm.)

## Language

- **Lead with recognition, not description.** Name the visitor's likely leak; don't describe
  Growmerce's services. (Pillar 1; `Pattern_Recognition_Experience.md`.)
- **Worldview statements as hooks.** Short, true, reframing lines (the beliefs, compressed).
- **Operator vocabulary throughout** (`Vocabulary_System.md`) — leak, conversion, margin, menu,
  rank, response flow.
- **Honest confidence wherever insight appears** — "we'd confirm this with your data."
- **Outcomes over features.** "Find where your sales leak," not "our AI-powered audit tool."
- **Arabic Fusha**, composed natively, operator register (`Arabic_Content_UX.md`).

## Examples

- Hero: "We find where your online sales leak — and operate the fixes." (not "We grow your
  ecommerce business.")
- Recognition: "Your discount lifts orders but quietly erases margin." (not "Improve your
  promotions.")
- Ecosystem: "We reason across your whole commerce surface — marketplaces, your store, delivery
  apps, social, WhatsApp." (not a logo wall caption.)
- Growth Operations: "We don't hand you a report and leave. We operate the fixes and show you the
  result." (Pillar 6.)
- Proof: "Here's a real finding — the evidence behind it, and how sure we are." (Pillar 3/4.)

## Things to avoid

- Generic value propositions ("we grow your sales", "your growth partner").
- Agency self-description ("full-service", "results-driven", "data-driven").
- Logo walls presented as borrowed credibility.
- Any "Words We Never Use" (hype, AI-magic, guarantees, manipulation, buzzwords).
- Claims without an evidence/confidence frame.
- Wall-of-CTA energy; one calm primary action per surface.

> The website's words are the first proof of the Intelligence Brain. Every line should make an
> operator think "finally, someone who gets it," and a sceptic lower their guard.
