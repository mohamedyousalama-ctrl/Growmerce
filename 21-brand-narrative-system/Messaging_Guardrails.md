# Messaging Guardrails

> The hard limits. Things Growmerce **never** says, on any surface, in any state, by any writer —
> human or AI. The guardrails are the enforcement layer of the narrative: where the worldview,
> vocabulary, and trust-language are positive guidance, these are the bright lines that, if
> crossed, break the brand and the trust it depends on.

This is the binding "never" list. It synthesises `What_Growmerce_Rejects.md`, the "Words We Never
Use" tier (`Vocabulary_System.md`), and the Brain's GUARD rules (20) into one reference.

---

## 1. Never guarantee outcomes

Commerce has too many variables to promise a number. Guarantees are dishonest and they signal
desperation.
- ❌ "We'll increase your sales by 30%." · "Guaranteed results." · "Risk-free growth."
- ✅ "This is, on the evidence, one of your highest-leverage opportunities — here's why."

## 2. Never fake certainty

Never claim to know more than the evidence supports. Overclaiming breaks the first time it's
wrong, and the audience is watching for exactly this.
- ❌ "We found the problem." (before evidence) · "This will definitely work."
- ✅ "The evidence strongly suggests…" · "We're highly confident, and here's the basis."

## 3. Never use hype

Hype is the language of people without evidence. It is the clearest agency/startup tell.
- ❌ explosive, skyrocket, 10x, supercharge, game-changing, revolutionary, crush it, dominate.
- ✅ specific, quantified-where-honest, calm operator language.

## 4. Never make AI-magic claims

AI is an instrument kept honest by evidence, never a magic box to invoke as proof.
- ❌ "Our AI knows." · "Powered by AI." (as a credential) · "Let AI grow your business."
- ✅ "The analysis points to… and here's the evidence and confidence behind it."

## 5. Never use manipulative urgency

Fake scarcity and pressure are beneath an operator-grade brand and instantly distrusted.
- ❌ "Limited slots." · "Act now." · "Last chance." · countdown pressure.
- ✅ a calm, standing invitation: "Talk to us when it's useful."

## 6. Never make vanity promises

Don't sell a feeling of progress detached from commercial outcome.
- ❌ "Go viral." · "Massive engagement." · "Transform your brand." (as empty uplift)
- ✅ outcomes tied to sales and profit, honestly sized.

## 7. Never fabricate findings or numbers

The one unforgivable act. Never invent a problem to justify a sale, or a statistic for drama.
(Directly from the Brain's GUARD, 20.)
- ❌ inventing a leak, a stat, or a benchmark not supported by evidence.
- ✅ "We can't determine this yet — here's what we'd need." (calibrated abstention)

## 8. Never hide uncertainty, assumptions, or data gaps

Concealing the edges of what we know to look smarter is a trust betrayal.
- ❌ a confident claim resting on an unstated assumption or a known data gap.
- ✅ "This assumes X; if that's off, the picture changes." · "We're missing Y to be sure."

## 9. Never celebrate revenue without profit

The agency tell, and a violation of the worldview.
- ❌ "Sales are up — success!" (with no margin view)
- ✅ "Sales are up. More importantly, here's what happened to profit."

## 10. Never talk down to or hype at the operator

The audience is sophisticated. Over-simplifying, buzzwording, or hyping insults them.
- ❌ buzzword fog (synergy, holistic, paradigm, world-class), or dumbed-down platitudes.
- ✅ plain, precise, respectful operator language.

## 11. Never use dark patterns

No manipulation in copy, capture, or CTAs — anywhere. A single dark pattern undoes the trust the
whole system builds (`04-website-ux`).
- ❌ disguised ads-as-content, forced steps, guilt/confirm-shaming, hidden costs.
- ✅ honest asks, clear value, easy exits.

## 12. Never sound like the four things we're not

No language that reads as a **marketing agency, generic consultant, dashboard/SaaS vendor, or
hype startup.** If a sentence could appear on any of their sites, rewrite it.

---

## How the guardrails are enforced

- **Human writers:** scan every draft against this list before publishing; any hit = rewrite.
- **AI generation (07/20):** the "Never Use" vocabulary and these rules are **hard filters** on
  generated content; the Brain's GUARD already forbids fabrication and overclaiming, so the
  narrative guardrails and the Brain's controls reinforce each other.
- **Review:** the guardrails are the first checklist in any content review, across every surface.

## The meta-rule

> When in doubt, say less, more honestly. Every guardrail reduces to one instinct: **never let the
> language promise, claim, or imply more than Growmerce can honestly stand behind.** That instinct,
> held everywhere, is what makes Growmerce sound like the one trustworthy voice in a noisy,
> over-promising category.

These are not stylistic preferences. They are the conditions under which the brand's central
asset — being believed — survives.
