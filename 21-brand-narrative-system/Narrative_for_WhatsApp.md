# Narrative for WhatsApp

> How Growmerce speaks on WhatsApp — the primary conversation channel
> (`04-website-ux/WhatsApp_and_Consultation_CTA.md`). WhatsApp is where the voice is **warmest and
> most human**, while still being the same honest, evidence-aware operator. It's a chat with a
> competent peer, not a support script or a marketing blast.

---

## Objective

Continue the relationship in the channel operators actually live in — turning a website finding
into a real conversation, and a conversation into a scoped engagement — with low friction and high
warmth.

## Tone

Warm, direct, conversational, responsive. A real person who knows their business, texting back
helpfully. Still calm and honest; never robotic, never salesy, never a wall of text.

## Language

- **Short, human messages.** One idea per message; conversational rhythm; no essays.
- **Context-aware from the first line** — reference what brought them here ("saw the
  discount-margin pattern in your store…"), so it starts warm.
- **Plain, peer register.** Slightly more relaxed than the website; can be lightly conversational
  while staying operator-grade. (Arabic: may flex warmer than strict Fusha — see Open Questions in
  04; keep it natural and respectful.)
- **Honest and specific**, even when brief — calibrated confidence still applies, just compressed
  ("pretty sure it's the checkout, not the price — want to confirm with your numbers?").
- **Useful before asking.** Give a real thought before requesting data or a call.
- **Clear, low-pressure next step** each time.

## Examples

- Opener: "Hey — saw you ran the diagnostic. The one that stood out: your discount is lifting
  orders but eating margin. Want me to pressure-test it on your real numbers?"
- Useful-first: "Quick thought before anything else — your bestseller might actually be losing
  money after shipping and fees. Worth a 10-min look."
- Honest: "Honestly, can't be sure from the link alone. If you can share rough order volume, I can
  tell you if the discounting is actually costing you."
- Next step: "If it's easier, send me your store link and I'll take a first look — no commitment."

## Things to avoid

- Long, formal, essay-style messages.
- Marketing-blast tone or copy-paste templates that ignore context.
- Pushy follow-ups, manufactured urgency, "just checking in!!" spam.
- Guarantees or hype (same guardrails as everywhere).
- Robotic support-script language.
- Asking for a call/commitment before being useful.

## The WhatsApp principle

> WhatsApp is the most personal channel Growmerce has, so it's where warmth is highest and
> friction is lowest — but the substance is identical to everywhere else: honest, evidence-aware,
> operator judgement. It should feel like texting a sharp friend who happens to run growth
> operations for a living.

> A good Growmerce WhatsApp exchange leaves the operator thinking "that was genuinely helpful and
> not pushy at all" — which is exactly what makes them continue it.
