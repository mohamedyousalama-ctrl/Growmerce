# What Growmerce Rejects

> The official rejection list — the things Growmerce refuses to do, say, or believe. A voice is
> defined as much by what it will not say as by what it says. Rejecting these consistently is what
> keeps Growmerce from sliding into the agency / hype / generic-AI register it exists to be
> different from.

Each rejection has a one-line reason. They are the inverse of the beliefs and the source of the
`Messaging_Guardrails.md`.

---

## Rejected metrics & thinking

1. **Vanity metrics.** Likes, impressions, and follower counts that don't connect to sales or
   profit are noise dressed as progress.
2. **Revenue as the headline number.** Top-line without margin is a half-truth, and often a
   dangerous one.
3. **Dashboard obsession.** Charts are not insight; staring at metrics is not analysis.
4. **Snapshot judgement.** Reading one day or one number and concluding — trends and context
   matter.
5. **Correlation paraded as causation.** "Sales went up after we did X" is a hypothesis, not a
   conclusion.

## Rejected advice & methods

6. **Generic advice.** "Improve your conversion / post more / lower your price" is uselessly vague.
7. **Frameworks without a business.** A consulting model applied without the operator's real
   numbers is theatre.
8. **Best-practice cargo-culting.** Copying what worked elsewhere without diagnosing this system.
9. **Growth hacks.** There is no trick; there is the constraint and the fix.
10. **Effort as a strategy.** "Just do more" ignores that the system, not the effort, is the limit.
11. **Symptom-chasing.** Treating "low conversion" as the problem instead of finding its cause.

## Rejected commercial behaviour

12. **Blind discounting.** Cutting price without measuring incrementality is giving away margin.
13. **Discounting to fix a non-price problem.** Masking a trust or clarity failure with a coupon.
14. **Racing to the bottom on price.** Especially against a structurally cheaper competitor.
15. **Acquisition-only growth.** Renting all your revenue and calling it a business.
16. **Treating the first sale as the finish line.** Ignoring the second purchase that makes the
    economics work.

## Rejected ways of speaking

17. **Hype.** "Explosive growth", "game-changing", "revolutionary" — words that promise what they
    can't back.
18. **Fake certainty.** Claiming to have "found the problem" before the evidence supports it.
19. **Manipulative urgency.** Fake scarcity, countdown pressure, "limited slots" — distrusted and
    beneath us.
20. **Guarantees of outcomes.** Commerce has too many variables; a guaranteed number is a lie.
21. **Buzzword fog.** "Synergy", "leverage", "holistic", "next-gen" — abstractions that hide the
    absence of a real point.
22. **AI-magic claims.** "Our AI knows" / "powered by AI" as a substitute for showing the evidence.
23. **Vanity promises.** Selling a feeling of progress instead of a commercial outcome.
24. **Talking down to the operator.** Over-simplifying or hyping insults a serious decision-maker.

## Rejected stances toward evidence & AI

25. **Recommendations without evidence.** Advice that can't show its reasoning is opinion.
26. **AI certainty without proof.** A confident model output with no traceable evidence is a
    hallucination risk.
27. **Hiding uncertainty.** Pretending to be sure when we're not is the fastest way to lose a
    sophisticated client.
28. **Fabricated findings.** Inventing a problem to justify a sale — the one unforgivable act.
29. **Withholding what's healthy.** Refusing to say what's *not* wrong because it doesn't sell.

## Rejected identities

30. **Acting like a marketing agency** — attention-first, persuasion-led, creativity-as-proof.
31. **Acting like a generic consultant** — frameworks and decks over operations and evidence.
32. **Acting like a SaaS/dashboard vendor** — handing over a tool instead of operating the fix.
33. **Acting like a hype-driven startup** — buzzwords, bravado, and promises over substance.

---

## How to use this list

- It is the source material for `Messaging_Guardrails.md` (the hard "never say" rules) and the
  "Words We Never Use" section of `Vocabulary_System.md`.
- Before publishing any Growmerce content, scan it against this list: if it does any of these
  things, rewrite it.
- Rejecting these is not negativity — it is discipline. Every rejection protects the perception
  that Growmerce understands online sales deeply and isn't trying to sell you something.

> Tell me what a brand refuses to say, and I'll tell you whether it has a point of view. This is
> Growmerce's refusal list — and it is half of the voice.
