# Vocabulary System

> The words. This is the mandatory vocabulary across the website, reports, TikTok, diagnostics,
> proposals, and consultations. Words carry worldview — "optimise your funnel" and "find where
> your checkout loses buyers" describe the same thing and signal completely different companies.
> Growmerce's word choices are a continuous trust signal.

Four tiers: **Words We Use · Words We Prefer · Words We Avoid · Words We Never Use.** Examples,
not exhaustive — the principle behind each tier is what's binding.

---

## 1. Words We Use (the native vocabulary)

The operator's working language — concrete commerce nouns and verbs that signal Growmerce has run
the operation. Use these freely; they are home territory.

**Diagnosis & system**
- leak, sales leak, profit leak · diagnose, diagnosis · system, operational system · constraint ·
  evidence, signal · pattern · confidence · finding · root cause

**Commerce operations**
- conversion, drop-off, friction · checkout, cart · listing, buy-box, rank, review velocity ·
  menu, availability, merchandising · response flow · fulfilment, returns · AOV, basket

**Economics**
- contribution, fully-loaded margin, true margin · incrementality · retention, repeat purchase,
  second purchase · lifetime value · acquisition cost · owned audience

**Action**
- operate, operate the fix · prioritise, sequence · quick win · execute · pressure-test · confirm

These words do double duty: they're accurate *and* they prove fluency. They map directly to the
patterns (19) and the Brain (20).

---

## 2. Words We Prefer (chosen over weaker alternatives)

Where a common word exists, Growmerce prefers the more precise, operator-grade, honest one.

| Prefer | Over | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **find / locate the leak** | "optimise" | concrete and diagnostic, not vague |
| **operate / execute** | "implement solutions" | we do the work, plainly said |
| **the evidence suggests** | "we think / we believe" | grounded, not opinion |
| **profit / contribution** | "revenue / growth" (when margin is meant) | honest about the real number |
| **diagnose** | "audit" | reasoning, not a checklist |
| **pattern** | "issue / problem" (generic) | specific and recognisable |
| **confidence: high/medium** | "definitely / probably" | calibrated, traceable |
| **operator / operating partner** | "agency / vendor / consultant" | our actual identity |
| **online sales** | "digital / ecommerce" (when broader) | the outcome the operator cares about |
| **clear offer** | "value proposition" | plain over jargon |
| **second purchase / repeat** | "loyalty" | measurable behaviour, not a feeling |
| **show you the result** | "drive impact" | concrete and provable |

The rule: when two words mean the same thing, choose the one a sharp operator would use and a
sceptic would trust.

## 3. Words We Avoid (use sparingly, with care)

Not banned, but they carry agency/startup baggage; use only when genuinely precise, never as
filler.

- **optimise / optimisation** — overused; prefer the specific change ("fix the checkout step").
- **solution / solutions** — vague vendor-speak; name the actual fix.
- **strategy** — fine when real; avoid as a deck-word substitute for operations.
- **engagement** — vanity-adjacent; tie to a sales/profit outcome or drop it.
- **funnel** — acceptable internally; on customer surfaces prefer "the path to purchase / the
  journey," concretely.
- **insights** — only if backed by evidence shown; otherwise it's filler.
- **data-driven** — show the data instead of claiming the adjective.
- **leverage (verb)** — prefer "use."
- **unlock** — fine occasionally ("unlock margin"); avoid as hype.

The test for this tier: am I using this word because it's *precise here*, or because it's a
reflex? If reflex, replace it with the concrete thing.

---

## 4. Words We Never Use (banned)

These break the voice and the worldview. They do not appear in Growmerce content, ever. (This tier
feeds `Messaging_Guardrails.md`.)

**Hype**
- explosive, skyrocket, supercharge, turbocharge, 10x, exponential, game-changer, revolutionary,
  disrupt, crush it, dominate, unleash, next-level, secret/hack

**Fake certainty & guarantees**
- guaranteed, guarantee results, surefire, proven to [outcome], we will increase your sales by X,
  risk-free returns on your business, "the problem is…" (before evidence)

**AI-magic**
- AI magic, our AI knows, powered by AI (as a proof), let AI do the work, magical, effortless,
  automatic growth

**Manipulation**
- limited slots, act now, don't miss out, last chance, only today, you can't afford not to

**Buzzword fog**
- synergy, holistic, paradigm, cutting-edge, world-class (self-applied), best-in-class
  (self-applied), seamless (as filler), ninja/rockstar/guru, thought leader

**Vanity**
- go viral (as a promise), blow up, massive growth (unquantified), transform your business
  (as an empty promise)

If any of these appears in a draft, the draft is wrong — rewrite the idea in operator language.

## 5. Special vocabulary notes

- **"Growth Operations"** — the name of the paid service; used precisely and consistently, never
  softened to "marketing" or "consulting."
- **"Commerce Intelligence"** — the engine; used to describe the reasoning, not as a buzzword.
- **Platform names** (Amazon, Noon, Talabat, Salla, Shopify, TikTok…) — used plainly and
  accurately; never as borrowed credibility.
- **Confidence words** — "high / medium / low confidence," "the evidence strongly suggests," "we
  can't yet determine" — these are load-bearing and governed by `Trust_Language_Framework.md`.
- **Arabic** — the Arabic equivalents of the Use/Prefer/Avoid/Never tiers are governed by
  `04-website-ux/Arabic_Content_UX.md`; the same worldview applies (operator Fusha, no hype, no
  cliché). The terminology spine (تسرّب المبيعات، الأنماط، عمليات النمو…) is shared.

---

## 6. How to use the vocabulary system

- It is **mandatory** across all surfaces and all writers (human and AI).
- When generating content, the AI agents (07/20) should treat "Never Use" as a hard filter and
  "Prefer over" as a rewrite rule.
- Vocabulary is the most granular layer of the voice — get the words right and the worldview comes
  through automatically; get them wrong and even good ideas sound like an agency.

> The fastest way to sound like a commerce operator instead of a marketer is to use the operator's
> words and refuse the marketer's. This system is that refusal, written down.
