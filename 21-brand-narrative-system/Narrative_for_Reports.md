# Narrative for Reports

> How Growmerce speaks in reports. A report is the Intelligence Brain explaining itself — an
> executive decision document, not a dashboard or a PDF
> (`04-website-ux/Report_Preview_Experience.md`). Its language must let a busy operator decide and
> act, and must read like the calmest, sharpest analyst they've ever hired.

---

## Objective

Communicate findings so the operator knows what's wrong, how sure Growmerce is, what it's costing
(in revenue *and* profit), what to do first, and that Growmerce can operate the fix — all without
overload or hype.

## Tone

Executive, precise, decision-first, calm. Confident where the evidence is strong; honest where it
isn't. Reads like a memo from someone who respects the reader's time and intelligence.

## Language

- **Decision-first.** Lead with the headline diagnosis (the one thing that matters most), then the
  system narrative (how the findings connect), then the prioritised findings.
- **Every finding is a claim scaled to evidence** (Trust Language grammar): finding + evidence +
  confidence + missing data + impact + effort + priority + recommended action + what Growmerce can
  execute.
- **Impact always split into revenue and profit** (Pillar 2), in honest ranges, never false
  precision.
- **Name what's healthy and what was ruled out** — credibility through balance.
- **Confidence on every finding**, in the four-band language (`Evidence_and_Confidence_Communication.md`).
- **Recommendations framed by confidence and leverage**, never as guarantees.

## Examples

- Headline: "If you change one thing: your bestseller is losing money after fully-loaded costs.
  Fixing its pricing recovers more margin than any growth tactic on this list."
- System narrative: "These aren't separate problems. You're discounting to drive volume on
  products that already lose money — every 'good month' deepens the hole."
- Finding (calibrated): "Discounting is likely eroding margin without adding sales. Discount
  penetration is high and margin is falling while revenue is flat. Moderate confidence — an
  incrementality test confirms it."
- Ruled out: "Your discovery and product quality are healthy. We're not worried about traffic.
  The leak is downstream."
- Execution: "What Growmerce would operate: restructure the discount rules and the loss-making
  SKUs' pricing, then prove the margin recovery in your numbers."

## Things to avoid

- Dashboard dumps — charts and metrics with no decision attached.
- PDF/brochure tone or filler.
- Listing 25 issues instead of the prioritised few.
- Revenue-only impact (the agency tell).
- Fabricated certainty or invented numbers.
- Hype, guarantees, or pressure language.

> A Growmerce report should make a CEO think "this is the clearest read of my business I've ever
> received — and they're honest about what they don't yet know." Decision-oriented, evidence-
> backed, calm.
