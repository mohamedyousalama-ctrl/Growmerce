# Narrative for Service Delivery

> How Growmerce speaks while delivering Growth Operations — the communication during the paid
> engagement (Operations Delivery, 12). This is where the narrative either proves true or collapses:
> the promise of "we operate the fix and prove it moved" (Pillar 6) is kept or broken in delivery
> communication. The voice here is **accountable, clear, and progress-focused.**

---

## Objective

Keep the client informed and confident during execution — communicating what's being operated,
what moved, what didn't, and what's next — so the engagement feels like a competent operating
partner at work, and so results are attributed honestly.

## Tone

Clear, accountable, calm, candid. Reports progress without spin; names what worked and what
didn't with equal honesty; never over-claims a result. Confident in the work, honest about the
outcomes.

## Language

- **Progress in terms of the system and the number.** "We changed X; here's how the signal moved."
- **Tie every action to the finding it fixes** and the success measure defined for it
  (Recommendation framework, 20) — close the loop the diagnosis opened.
- **Honest attribution.** Distinguish what moved from confounders; don't claim credit the evidence
  doesn't support (the same calibration as everywhere — `Trust_Language_Framework.md`).
- **Name what didn't work plainly** and what you're doing about it. A fix that didn't land is
  reported, not hidden.
- **Separate revenue from profit** in results, always (Pillar 2).
- **Calm cadence.** Steady, predictable updates; no breathless "huge win!!" energy even for good
  results.

## Examples

- Update: "This week we restructured the discount rules on the loss-making SKUs. Early signal:
  blended margin is up, orders held flat — which is exactly what we wanted. Still early; we'll
  confirm over the next two weeks."
- Honest miss: "The checkout change didn't move conversion as much as we expected. The data points
  to a second friction we missed at payment — we're fixing that next."
- Attribution: "Margin's up 6% this month. About two-thirds of that we can attribute to the
  pricing fix; the rest overlaps with your seasonal lift, so we won't claim all of it."
- Profit framing: "Revenue is flat this month — but profit is up, because we stopped selling the
  items that lost money. That's the result we were after."

## Things to avoid

- Spin or vanity-metric reporting ("engagement is up!") detached from sales/profit.
- Over-claiming results or taking credit for confounded gains.
- Hiding or burying what didn't work.
- Hype language even on genuine wins ("massive", "explosive").
- Vague updates with no link to a finding or a number.
- Going quiet when results are slow — candor beats silence.

## Why delivery narrative matters most

> Everything before delivery is a promise; delivery is where the promise is tested. If the
> communication during execution is honest, accountable, and tied to evidence, it *confirms*
> everything the brand said earlier and earns retention and referral. If it spins or over-claims,
> it retroactively makes the whole narrative feel like marketing after all. Delivery is where
> Growmerce's worldview is either proven or exposed.

> The client should finish each update thinking "they're doing the work, they're honest about what
> moved, and they don't oversell" — the same operator they met on day one, now proving it.
