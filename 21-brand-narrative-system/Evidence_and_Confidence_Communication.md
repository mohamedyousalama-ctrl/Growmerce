# Evidence & Confidence Communication

> The wiring between the narrative and the machinery — how the language connects to the
> Intelligence Brain's confidence levels and evidence hierarchy (20) and the Pattern Engine (19).
> The `Trust_Language_Framework.md` defines *how to phrase* trust; this document defines *what the
> underlying system requires* and how confidence and evidence should appear across diagnostics,
> reports, recommendations, and consultations.

---

## 1. The principle: language must reflect the machine

Growmerce's honesty is not a stylistic affectation — it is **structurally true.** The Brain (20)
actually computes confidence and tiers evidence; the narrative simply tells that truth. This means
the language is always backed by something real, which is exactly why it's trustworthy.

```
Brain computes:   evidence (tiered) → confidence (4 bands) → finding
Narrative speaks: "here's what we see, here's how we know, here's how sure we are"
```

The rule: **never communicate more confidence than the Brain computed, and never assert a finding
the evidence doesn't support.** The narrative is bound by the machine.

---

## 2. The evidence hierarchy, in language

The Brain tiers evidence (20: Signal & Evidence Hierarchy). The narrative communicates which tier
a claim rests on, in plain terms:

| Evidence tier (20) | How the narrative refers to it |
|---|---|
| **Tier 3 — hard data** (orders, analytics, costs) | "your data shows / your numbers show" |
| **Tier 2 — derived/observed** (a live URL, an estimate, an inference) | "from your store/listing we can see / this points to" |
| **Tier 1 — self-reported/anecdote** | "you mentioned / it sounds like" — explicitly held as a starting point, not proof |

Key narrative move: when a claim rests only on Tier 1, **say so and say what would upgrade it** —
"that's what you're seeing; let's confirm it with the actual numbers." This is the
honesty-as-funnel mechanic (`Trust_Language_Framework.md`).

The convergence principle is also spoken: *"one number could be a fluke; three signals agreeing is
a pattern"* — communicating *why* multiple signals raise confidence (heuristic H4, 20).

---

## 3. The four confidence bands, communicated consistently

The Brain's four bands map to fixed narrative registers (also in Trust Language; repeated here as
the canonical reference):

| Band | When (per 20) | Narrative register | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Very High** | Tier 3 + convergence + counter-evidence ruled out | definitive, plain | "The data clearly shows your bestseller loses money after true costs." |
| **High** | strong evidence, counter-checked | strong, attributed | "The evidence strongly indicates a checkout-trust gap." |
| **Medium** | suggestive, not yet confirmed | suspicion + path | "We strongly suspect discounting is eroding margin — an incrementality test confirms it." |
| **Low** | thin / single-signal | early signal, not a finding | "There's an early signal here worth checking, nothing conclusive yet." |

A finding is only *surfaced as a finding* at Medium or above (the Brain's client-facing floor).
Low-confidence items are spoken as "signals to check," never as conclusions — language enforces
the same floor the Brain enforces.

---

## 4. Where confidence and evidence appear (by surface)

### In diagnostics
- Every surfaced pattern carries its confidence band, in plain words, as it's shown.
- The diagnostic openly shows **what it's drawing on** and **what it's missing** ("with your link
  we can assess X; for Y we'd need Z").
- Confidence rises visibly as the user adds evidence — the language tracks it ("now that you've
  added order data, we can speak to profit with high confidence").

### In reports
- Every finding shows: the finding, the **evidence** behind it, the **confidence** band, and the
  **missing data** that would raise it (per `04-website-ux/Report_Preview_Experience.md`).
- Impact is always split into revenue and profit (Pillar 2).
- The report names **what it ruled out** and **what it couldn't assess** — confidence about the
  boundaries of the analysis, not just the findings.

### In recommendations
- Each recommendation inherits its finding's confidence and says so: "highest-confidence move…",
  "lower-confidence but high-upside, we'd test it first."
- Recommendations are framed by leverage and confidence, never as guarantees (Trust Language §3).

### In consultations
- The human operator speaks the same calibrated language live: stating confidence, naming
  assumptions, distinguishing what's confirmed from what's suspected, and proposing the test that
  would settle an open question.
- This consistency between the automated output and the human voice is itself a trust signal.

---

## 5. Communicating patterns (19) with evidence

When the narrative names a pattern, it carries the pattern's evidence structure:
- **The pattern** in operator words (the recognition).
- **The evidence signals** that triggered it ("how we'd know").
- **The confidence** and any **counter-evidence** considered ("what would tell us we're wrong").

This is what makes Growmerce's pattern recognition feel like intelligence rather than a horoscope
(see `04-website-ux/Pattern_Recognition_Experience.md`): every recognition is falsifiable and
evidence-aware in how it's spoken.

---

## 6. The non-negotiables (bound to the Brain's GUARD, 20)

The narrative is forbidden from:
- Asserting a finding the evidence doesn't support (no fabrication — the Brain's GUARD rule,
  spoken).
- Stating higher confidence than computed.
- Hiding an assumption or a data gap to sound more certain.
- Presenting a Tier-1 anecdote as proof.
- Claiming AI certainty without traceable evidence.

These are the same prohibitions the Brain enforces internally; the narrative makes them audible.
When the machine abstains ("we can't determine this yet"), the language abstains too — honestly,
and with a path forward.

---

## 7. Why this is a differentiator, not just a discipline

Most companies communicate conclusions; Growmerce communicates **conclusions plus their
evidentiary basis and confidence.** That extra layer is only possible because the Brain actually
produces it — competitors who bolt on "confidence scores" without the underlying reasoning will
sound hollow, because the language won't be backed by a real machine. The integrity between what
Growmerce *computes* and what it *says* is the moat. Evidence-and-confidence communication is where
the narrative system and the Intelligence Brain become one thing.
