# Narrative by User State

> The voice, applied to the user-state journey from Workstream 04. The personality is constant,
> but *what* Growmerce says and *how* it says it changes with the visitor's state of certainty.
> This document gives the communication goal, message style, language examples, and CTA style for
> each state — Curiosity → Recognition → Diagnosis → Evidence → Trust → Action → Commitment →
> Return.

This is the narrative spine that the per-surface documents inherit. (States from
`04-website-ux/User_State_Journey.md`; Return from
`04-website-ux/Intelligence_Retention_and_Return_Loops.md`.)

---

## The principle: speak to the certainty they have, toward the certainty they need

Each state is a different level of conviction. The narrative meets them where they are and earns
the next step. Saying a "Trust"-state thing to a "Curiosity"-state visitor (or vice versa) breaks
the journey.

---

## State 1 — Curiosity · "I think something is wrong."

- **User mindset:** vague unease; sales aren't growing, or growth doesn't feel profitable; sceptical, scanning.
- **Communication goal:** earn the first few seconds; make them feel this is *different* and *for them*.
- **Message style:** sharp, true, slightly provocative; a worldview statement that reframes their problem.
- **Language examples:**
  - "Most businesses don't have a traffic problem. They have a leak problem."
  - "A great month and a profitable month aren't the same thing."
  - "Your sales aren't low because demand is low. Demand is escaping somewhere."
- **CTA style:** low-commitment, curiosity-led — "See where your sales leak."

## State 2 — Recognition · "I think this problem is happening to me."

- **User mindset:** "wait — that's us." Curiosity becoming personal.
- **Communication goal:** name their exact leak in their own felt words (pattern recognition, 19).
- **Message style:** specific, operator-true, in their language; recognition over explanation.
- **Language examples:**
  - "Your discount increases orders but quietly erases your margin."
  - "Traffic reaches your page, but trust isn't strong enough to close the sale."
  - "Your delivery menu is hiding the items that should be selling."
- **CTA style:** test-it invitation — "See if this is happening in your business."

## State 3 — Diagnosis · "Show me where the leak is."

- **User mindset:** willing to be analysed; wants the specific answer; wary of forms and time-wasting.
- **Communication goal:** make the analysis feel intelligent and effortless; show the Brain working.
- **Message style:** guiding, precise, collaborative; explain what we're looking at and why.
- **Language examples:**
  - "Tell us where you sell and what — we'll find where it's leaking."
  - "Paste your store link. We'll start with what we can see."
  - "We're checking discovery, conversion, and margin — in that order."
- **CTA style:** progress-oriented, optional — "Add your link to go deeper (optional)."

## State 4 — Evidence · "Prove it."

- **User mindset:** interested but unconvinced; "show me you're not guessing."
- **Communication goal:** back every claim with evidence and honest confidence (Trust Language).
- **Message style:** evidence-first, calibrated; claim + how-we-know + how-sure.
- **Language examples:**
  - "Here's what the evidence shows — and how we know."
  - "Three signals point the same way: rank is slipping, recent reviews have stalled, and a competitor is gaining."
  - "We're highly confident here. Less sure there — and here's what would settle it."
- **CTA style:** continue-the-analysis — "See the full picture."

## State 5 — Trust · "These people understand my business."

- **User mindset:** belief forming; testing whether it's real understanding or generic.
- **Communication goal:** demonstrate operator-grade judgement — the system narrative, ruled-out items, profit≠revenue.
- **Message style:** calm, authoritative-but-honest, connective ("here's the shape of what's going on").
- **Language examples:**
  - "You don't have three problems. You have one fragile growth model — here's how the parts connect."
  - "Your discovery and product are actually strong. The leak is at checkout. That's good news — it's fixable."
  - "We won't tell you everything's broken. Most of this is healthy. Two things aren't."
- **CTA style:** confident, low-pressure — "Here's what we'd do first."

## State 6 — Action · "What should I do now?"

- **User mindset:** convinced there's a real problem; wants a clear, prioritised path.
- **Communication goal:** give one prioritised, executable first move, framed by confidence and leverage.
- **Message style:** decisive, practical, sequenced; no overwhelm.
- **Language examples:**
  - "If you do one thing this week: fix the checkout step losing buyers. Highest confidence, fastest payback."
  - "Then, in order: rebuild the offer clarity, then test a premium tier."
  - "This is what we'd operate — and how we'd prove it moved."
- **CTA style:** action-anchored — "See the prioritised plan / talk it through."

## State 7 — Commitment · "Let's talk."

- **User mindset:** ready to engage a human; wants substance, dreads a vague sales call.
- **Communication goal:** make the conversation feel specific, valuable, and safe (an operator session, not a pitch).
- **Message style:** warm, concrete, peer-to-peer; name what the conversation will produce.
- **Language examples:**
  - "Send your findings to WhatsApp — we'll pressure-test them against your real numbers."
  - "We'll tell you the one or two things we'd fix first, and what it would take."
  - "No pitch. We'll show you what we see and how we'd operate it."
- **CTA style:** specific conversation — "Talk to Growmerce on WhatsApp."

## State 8 — Return · "I want to come back."

- **User mindset:** already trusts Growmerce; returns for what changed, what's new, how they compare.
- **Communication goal:** reward the return with new, real intelligence (Retention Loops, 04).
- **Message style:** continuity + freshness; "here's what moved," "here's what's new for you."
- **Language examples:**
  - "Since last time, your marketplace rank moved — here's what changed and why."
  - "You haven't acted on two of the opportunities we found. Here's the highest-impact one."
  - "Here's how your position compares to similar businesses now."
- **CTA style:** progress-oriented — "Pick up where you left off / see what changed."

## The arc as one voice

```
1 Curiosity   reframe the problem        (worldview)
2 Recognition name it precisely          (patterns)
3 Diagnosis   analyse, visibly           (the Brain working)
4 Evidence    prove it, calibrated       (trust language)
5 Trust       show the system + honesty  (operator judgement)
6 Action      one prioritised move       (recommendations)
7 Commitment  a real, safe conversation  (operator session)
8 Return      reward the relationship    (living intelligence)
```

Across all eight, the personality never changes — analytical, evidence-based, calm, direct,
honest. Only the *certainty being addressed* changes. Get the state right, and the right words
follow.
