# Narrative for TikTok

> How Growmerce speaks in short-form video. TikTok is where Curiosity and Recognition are won at
> speed (`04-website-ux/Mobile_TikTok_Landing_Experience.md`). The voice gets *sharper and faster*
> here — but never abandons evidence, calm, or honesty for hype. This is the hardest balance in
> the system: attention-grabbing without becoming the hype-driven content the brand rejects.

---

## Objective

Stop the scroll, deliver one true, recognisable commerce insight, and make the viewer think
"that's my business" — driving them to a landing page in the same state of mind the lander
continues.

## Tone

Sharp, direct, confident, a little provocative — but grounded. The energy of a smart operator
telling you something true that most people get wrong, not an influencer hyping a hack. Calm
authority, faster cadence.

## Language

- **One pattern per video.** Each video dramatises a single pattern/belief (19; `What_Growmerce_Believes.md`).
- **Hook with a reframe or a hard truth**, not a promise. The belief list is the hook bank.
- **Concrete operator specifics**, not abstractions — a real number, a real mechanism, a real
  example.
- **Evidence-aware even when fast** — "here's why" beats "trust me." No fabricated stats.
- **Honest about nuance** where it matters ("this isn't always true, but when it is, it's
  expensive").
- **End on recognition + a low-friction next step**, matching the lander's hook exactly (continuity).

## Examples (hooks / framings)

- "Your bestseller might be your biggest loss. Here's how that happens." (Pillar 2)
- "Stop blaming your traffic. Most sales problems aren't a traffic problem." (Pillar 1/5)
- "Your discount isn't growing your business. It's funding your customers' discount habit." (belief 15)
- "Your competitor isn't winning on price. They're winning because their offer is clearer." (belief 19)
- "Three signs your marketplace rank is rented, not earned — and why it'll collapse." (Marketplace patterns)

## Things to avoid

- Hype hooks ("This ONE trick will EXPLODE your sales!").
- Guarantees or fabricated stats for drama.
- Growth-hack framing — Growmerce rejects hacks (belief: there are constraints and fixes, not
  tricks).
- Generic motivational/business-bro content.
- Manipulative urgency or clickbait that the landing can't honour (breaks continuity + trust).
- Buzzwords and influencer clichés.

## The TikTok tension, resolved

TikTok rewards boldness; Growmerce demands honesty. The resolution: **be bold about the truth, not
about the promise.** A provocative *reframe* of how commerce really works stops the scroll without
lying. Growmerce's worldview is genuinely contrarian to common marketing wisdom — that contrarian
truth *is* the hook. No hype required.

> On TikTok, Growmerce is the operator who says the uncomfortable, specific, true thing the
> hype-merchants won't — fast enough to stop the scroll, honest enough to keep the trust.
