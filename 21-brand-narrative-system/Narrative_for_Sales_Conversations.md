# Narrative for Sales Conversations

> How Growmerce speaks in sales conversations and consultations. This is where Commitment is
> earned (`Narrative_by_User_State.md`, State 7). The defining principle: a Growmerce sales
> conversation should **feel like a working session with an operator, not a pitch.** The best
> sales motion is to keep diagnosing — selling happens as a by-product of being obviously useful.

---

## Objective

Move a warm prospect from "let's talk" to a scoped Growth Operations engagement — by continuing
the intelligence (fast-judgement → deep diagnosis), demonstrating operator-grade judgement live,
and framing the work honestly.

## Tone

Collaborative, curious, calm, confident. A peer who has run businesses, not a closer. Listens
more than pitches; diagnoses more than persuades. Comfortable with "I don't know yet."

## Language

- **Lead with their business, not ours.** Ask the questions a sharp operator would; reason out
  loud from their answers.
- **Pressure-test, don't pitch.** "Let's test that against your real numbers" is the core move.
- **Calibrated confidence live** — state what's confirmed vs. suspected; name assumptions; propose
  the test that settles an open question (Trust Language, spoken).
- **Separate revenue from profit** in every diagnosis (Pillar 2) — it's the moment they realise
  Growmerce is different.
- **Frame the offer as operating, not advising** (Pillar 6): "we don't hand you a list — we do the
  work and prove it moved."
- **Name what you'd do first and why** — prioritised, sequenced, honest about effort.

## Examples

- Opening: "Before I tell you anything, walk me through where you sell and what's been frustrating
  you." (diagnose first)
- Reframe: "That sounds like a traffic problem, but the numbers suggest it's a conversion problem.
  Can we look?"
- Honesty: "I'm confident your margin is leaking on this. I'm not yet sure whether it's the
  discounting or the shipping — here's how we'd find out."
- Profit moment: "Your revenue's up, which is why this feels good. But after fully-loaded costs,
  this line is losing money. That's the real conversation."
- Offer: "Here's what we'd operate, in what order, and how we'd prove each fix moved the number.
  No guarantees — but this is where the evidence says the leverage is."

## Things to avoid

- Pitch-mode monologue; talking more than listening.
- Guarantees or outcome promises (banned — `Messaging_Guardrails.md`).
- Manufactured urgency or scarcity to close.
- Overclaiming certainty to win the deal — the fastest way to lose a serious operator.
- Feature-listing or "why choose us" energy.
- Discounting the offer to close (Growmerce rejects blind discounting — including on itself).

## The sales philosophy

> The conversation *is* the demonstration. If Growmerce diagnoses honestly and usefully in the
> room, the prospect concludes on their own that they want Growmerce to operate the fixes. Selling
> by being useful is slower per conversation and far higher-trust — which is exactly right for a
> high-consideration operating relationship.

> A prospect should leave a Growmerce sales conversation thinking "that was genuinely useful, and
> they were honest about what they don't know" — whether or not they buy today.
