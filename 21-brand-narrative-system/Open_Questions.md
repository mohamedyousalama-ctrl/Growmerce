# Open Questions — Brand Narrative System

Unresolved questions for the narrative system. Surfaced deliberately; none block the system, all
inform brand identity (03), content production, and the AI agents that will generate language.

---

## Voice & personality

1. **Voice as a person — named or unnamed?** Does Growmerce speak as an institutional "we," a
   first-person operator persona, or both depending on surface (WhatsApp = personal, reports =
   institutional)? Needs a clear rule.
2. **Humour and warmth budget.** The personality is calm and direct. How much dry wit/warmth is
   on-brand, especially on TikTok and WhatsApp, without tipping into "cute" (which we reject)?
3. **Founder voice vs. brand voice.** Early on, much content is the founder's voice. How do we
   codify the founder's natural register into the system so it survives a team and AI generation?

## Trust language

4. **Confidence-band wording lock.** The four-band spoken forms ("the data clearly shows" / "the
   evidence strongly indicates" / "we strongly suspect" / "an early signal") should be locked as
   canonical phrases. Do we standardise exact wording, or keep them as patterns to vary?
5. **Hedging fatigue.** If every claim is calibrated, does the constant hedging language become
   repetitive or read as uncertain in aggregate? How do we vary it while keeping the discipline?
6. **Confidence in TikTok.** Fast content has little room for full calibration. What's the minimum
   honest framing that fits a 30-second video without overclaiming?

## Vocabulary & Arabic

7. **Bilingual vocabulary lock.** The Use/Prefer/Avoid/Never tiers need their Arabic equivalents
   finalised with a native commerce writer, consistent with `04-website-ux/Arabic_Content_UX.md`.
   Who owns and maintains the shared terminology spine?
8. **WhatsApp Arabic register.** Does WhatsApp flex from Fusha toward a warmer, lightly-colloquial
   Arabic while the rest of the site stays Fusha? (Shared with 04 Open Question.) Where's the line?
9. **Platform/term transliteration.** Consistent rules for rendering platform names and commerce
   terms across Arabic content.

## Consistency & enforcement

10. **AI generation conformance.** How do we encode the worldview, vocabulary, trust-language, and
    guardrails tightly enough that AI-generated diagnostic/report language reliably stays on-voice
    at scale? Is there a machine-checkable subset (banned words, required confidence framing)?
11. **Review workflow.** Who is the voice owner/editor, and what's the lightweight review gate for
    each surface so consistency holds as volume grows?
12. **Drift detection.** How do we notice voice drift over time (e.g. content slowly getting more
    agency-like) and correct it?

## Scope & handoff

13. **Brand identity handoff (03).** Which narrative elements directly constrain the visual system
    (e.g. "calm, not flashy" implies restraint), and which are independent? What's the explicit
    brief from narrative to identity?
14. **Sales/delivery training.** The narrative for sales, WhatsApp, and delivery is human-spoken.
    How is it taught and reinforced with the people (not just documented)?
15. **Future SaaS voice.** When Growmerce becomes partly self-serve product, does the voice change
    (less "we operate," more "the product shows you")? How does the worldview translate to product
    UI copy without losing the operator identity?
