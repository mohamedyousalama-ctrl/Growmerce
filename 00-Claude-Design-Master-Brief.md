# 00 — Claude Design Master Brief

> **The single source of truth for Claude Design.** Read only this document and you will fully
> understand Growmerce — what it is, why it exists, why it can be trusted, how it thinks, how it
> learns, what it must feel like, and what must never happen. You do not need to read the underlying

This is not strategy and not design. It is the **translation layer** between the entire Growmerce
architecture (eight workstreams) and the visual and interaction system you will create.

How to use it: Sections 01–10 build understanding (what Growmerce is and how it thinks). Sections
11–15 translate that into design philosophy. Sections 16–18 are the operational guide (must-haves,

## 01 — Growmerce In One Sentence

> **Growmerce finds where a business's online sales and profit are leaking — with evidence, honestly
> scored for confidence — and operates the fixes.**

That is the literal truth, not a slogan. Every word matters to design:
- **finds** — it is diagnostic; it discovers things the operator can't see.
- **online sales and profit** — across marketplaces, stores, delivery apps, social, WhatsApp; and it
  separates *profit* from *sales* (most don't).
- **leaking** — the core metaphor: existing demand and margin escaping through fixable holes.
- **with evidence, honestly scored for confidence** — it proves what it says and tells you how sure
  it is. This is the trust engine.
- **operates the fixes** — it doesn't advise and leave; it does the work. It is an operator, not a
  consultant.

Arabic name: **جرومرس**. Positioning line: **Online Sales Growth Operations, powered by Commerce
Intelligence.**

## 02 — What Growmerce Is

Growmerce is four things at once. Design must honour all four.

**An intelligence company.** Its core asset is *understanding* — a structured, compounding body of
commercial knowledge and a reasoning engine that turns observations into trustworthy conclusions. It
is built like Bloomberg, Moody's, or a credit bureau: an institution that produces *trusted
intelligence*, not content or software.

**A commerce intelligence system.** It reasons specifically about online commerce — marketplaces,
ecommerce, delivery, pricing, promotion, conversion, retention, competition, operations. It
recognises recurring commercial realities ("patterns") and names the operator's exact problem.

**A growth operations system.** The paid product is *operating the fixes* — Growth Operations. Free
diagnostics, tools, and reports are proof of expertise and lead magnets, never the product itself.

**A decision support system.** Its output is a prioritised, evidence-backed, confidence-scored set of
decisions an operator can act on — "here is what's wrong, how sure we are, what it's costing you, and
what to do first."

For design: it is an **instrument a serious operator uses to understand and grow their business** —
closer to an intelligence terminal than a marketing site, but rendered with calm and clarity, not
density.

## 03 — What Growmerce Is NOT

This section is critical. Growmerce will constantly be mistaken for things it is not, and the design's
first job is to *not look like any of them.* Each "not" is a visual and behavioural trap.

| Not a… | Because… | The design trap to avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Marketing agency** | it doesn't sell attention or creativity; it diagnoses and operates | hero photography, gradient hype, "we grow brands," portfolio walls |
| **Dashboard** | it produces conclusions, not metric displays; it interprets, you don't | dark metric walls, chart grids, "data overload" |
| **Reporting company** | it diagnoses and acts; a report is a decision brief, not a deliverable | static PDF feel, document-dump aesthetics |
| **AI wrapper** | it reasons over verified evidence and knowledge, not model guesses | glowing AI, sparkles, "magic," robot/brain imagery |
| **Consultancy** | it operates the fix; it doesn't hand over a deck | framework diagrams, stiff corporate-consulting visuals |
| **Marketplace agency** | it reasons across the whole commerce ecosystem, not one platform | single-platform tool aesthetics |
| **Analytics tool** | it tells you what to *do*, not just what *happened* | "explore your data" tooling, query-builder vibes |
| **BI platform** | it reaches conclusions for you; BI makes you do the thinking | pivot-table density, self-serve-report builders |

> The unifying rule: **Growmerce understands and decides; the things it is not merely display,
> persuade, or advise.** Anything that makes it look like it only displays (dashboard/BI/analytics),
> only persuades (agency/startup), or only advises (consultancy) is wrong.

## 04 — User Psychology

**Who the user is.** Serious online-commerce operators and decision-makers: restaurant/delivery
operators, retail operators, commerce managers, marketplace managers, founders, regional managers,
commercial directors, social-commerce sellers. Arabic-first market. They run real businesses with
real money at stake.

**Their emotional state.** Busy. Sceptical. Tired. They have been pitched by agencies, sold dashboards
they don't use, and given generic advice that didn't work. They scan; they don't read. They trust
precision and operational fluency, and they instantly detect marketing fluff and hype.

**Their fears:**
- "I'm losing sales/money and I don't know why."
- "Another agency will waste my time and money."
- "I'll be sold something generic dressed up as insight."
- "An AI will confidently tell me nonsense."
- "I'll hand over my data and get a vague consultation call."

**Their frustrations:** sales that won't grow; growth that isn't profitable; not knowing which channel
is the problem; competitors who seem to be winning; advice that's obvious or wrong.

**Their skepticism:** they discount claims automatically. They believe only what they can see
demonstrated. Testimonials and logo walls do not move them.

**Their mental models:** they think operationally (stock, conversion, margin, menus, reviews,
response time), not in marketing abstractions. They respect someone who has clearly *run* a business.

**Their objections:** "How do you know?" · "Is this just AI?" · "Why are you different from an agency?"
· "Can you actually prove this?" · "What will you actually do?"

**What they believe BEFORE Growmerce:** *"This is probably another agency/tool/AI that will overpromise
and underdeliver. I'll be skeptical until proven otherwise."*

**What they believe AFTER trusting Growmerce:** *"These people understand my online sales better than I
do. They showed me my exact problem with evidence, were honest about what they weren't sure of, and
they can actually fix it. This isn't an agency — it's a commerce intelligence operating system."*

The entire design exists to move the user from the first belief to the second. **Design for the
sceptic, not the enthusiast.**

## 05 — User State Journey

The website is organised around **user states and cognitive transitions, not pages.** A user moves
through states of progressive certainty. Pages are merely containers that earn the next transition.
This is the backbone of the whole experience — design every surface around *which state the user is in
and which state they must leave in.*

For each state: mindset · desired emotion · friction to remove · evidence needed · visual implications.

### 1. Curiosity — "I think something is wrong."
- **Mindset:** vague unease; sales flat or growth unprofitable; scanning, skeptical.
- **Desired emotion:** intrigued recognition that *this is different.*
- **Friction:** "another agency / not for me." → removed by instant operator-grade specificity.
- **Evidence needed:** none yet — a sharp, true reframe of their problem.
- **Visual:** a calm, confident opening that reframes ("most businesses don't have a traffic problem,
  they have a leak problem"); no hero-banner hype.

### 2. Recognition — "This is happening to me."
- **Mindset:** "wait… that's us."
- **Desired emotion:** being *seen* precisely.
- **Friction:** skepticism that "they don't know my business." → removed by naming the exact leak in
  the operator's own words.
- **Evidence needed:** a recognisable *pattern* stated plainly (not a category).
- **Visual:** the **recognition card** — the pattern in the operator's felt language, primary; calm
  frame, words are the hero.

### 3. Diagnosis — "Show me where the leak is."
- **Mindset:** willing to be analysed; wary of forms and wasted time.
- **Desired emotion:** watching a real intelligence reason about *their* business.
- **Friction:** form fatigue, upload anxiety, time fear. → removed by a command-style input,
  value-first flow, everything optional.
- **Evidence needed:** the system visibly *using* their input.
- **Visual:** the **command surface** (Spotlight/Raycast feel) + suggestion cards (accept/reject/edit/
  add) + editable chips + an honest "working" state, not a fake spinner.

### 4. Understanding / Evidence — "Prove it."
- **Mindset:** interested but unconvinced; "show me you're not guessing."
- **Desired emotion:** "they can actually back this up."
- **Friction:** "AI nonsense / made up." → removed by showing evidence + honest confidence + what's
  missing.
- **Evidence needed:** the signals behind the finding, the confidence level, the counter-checks.
- **Visual:** the **evidence/confidence language** — proof shown, confidence as a calm honest scale,
  missing-data shown openly.

### 5. Trust — "These people understand my business."
- **Mindset:** belief forming; testing whether it's real or generic.
- **Desired emotion:** calm conviction.
- **Friction:** "sounds smart but maybe generic." → removed by the *system narrative* (how findings
  connect), ruled-out items ("what's NOT wrong"), and profit-vs-revenue separation.
- **Evidence needed:** operator-grade judgement, demonstrated.
- **Visual:** the **executive decision brief** — calm, prioritised, honest; the most important trust
  surface.

### 6. Action — "What should I do now?"
- **Mindset:** convinced; wants a clear, prioritised path.
- **Desired emotion:** clarity and control, not overwhelm.
- **Friction:** "now what / too much." → removed by one prioritised first action and a clear sequence.
- **Evidence needed:** the recommendation, framed by confidence and leverage.
- **Visual:** the **prioritised plan** — "if you do one thing," then Now/Next/Plan, decisive hierarchy.

### 7. Commitment — "Let's talk."
- **Mindset:** ready to engage a human; dreads a vague sales call.
- **Desired emotion:** safe, specific, peer-to-peer.
- **Friction:** vague-call dread, agency distrust. → removed by a context-aware, specific WhatsApp
  conversation ("we'll pressure-test these on your real numbers").
- **Evidence needed:** the conversation is clearly substantive, not a pitch.
- **Visual:** **WhatsApp-first**, persistent, context-carrying; warmest register.

### 8. Retention / Long-Term Relationship — "I want to come back."
- **Mindset:** already trusts; returns for what changed, what's new, how they compare.
- **Desired emotion:** an ongoing intelligence relationship, not a one-off report.
- **Friction:** "I already got my report." → removed by progressive disclosure and real change
  (their data moved, a new pattern emerged, an opportunity is unactioned).
- **Evidence needed:** new, real intelligence on return.
- **Visual:** a living surface — saved profile, opportunity tracker, benchmarks, "what changed." The
  outcome we engineer is *"I want to come back,"* not *"I downloaded a report."*

> Design rule: **never skip a state.** The classic failure is jumping from Curiosity straight to
> Commitment ("book a call"). Every transition is *earned* by showing the user something, never
> assumed.

## 06 — The Intelligence Philosophy

Growmerce's intelligence is a chain of distinct concepts. A designer must understand them because the
interface *renders* them — and confusing them produces a generic product.

| Concept | Plain meaning | In designer language |
|---|---|---|
| **Signal** | a raw observation about the business (a stockout, a 1.4% conversion) | the raw input; humble, un-interpreted |
| **Evidence** | a *verified, trusted* signal, with a source quality | proof; always shown with its source and freshness |
| **Knowledge** | what's generally true about commerce (benchmarks, principles, playbooks) | the standing expertise that gives a number meaning |
| **Pattern** | a recurring, recognisable commercial reality ("Review Density Gap") | the recognition moment; the named problem |
| **Confidence** | how sure the system is, honestly (Low→Very High) | a calm, honest scale attached to every conclusion |
| **Reasoning** | how the system connects evidence + knowledge into a finding | a visible, trustworthy thought process, not magic |
| **Recommendation** | a prioritised action, from a validated playbook | the decision; clear, sequenced, justified |
| **Human review** | an expert verifies high-stakes conclusions | a quiet trust signal ("reviewed by a specialist") |
| **Trust** | the cumulative result of all of the above being honest | the actual conversion engine (Section 10) |

**How they connect (the spine — memorise this):**

```
SIGNAL → (verified into) EVIDENCE → (interpreted by) KNOWLEDGE → (recognised as) PATTERN
   → (scored by) CONFIDENCE → (turned into) RECOMMENDATION → (checked by) HUMAN REVIEW → TRUST
```

The single most important design translation: **the interface must make this chain *visible and
honest.*** The user should always be able to feel *what the system observed, why it believes it, how
sure it is, and what to do* — never just a confident answer with no basis. That visible chain is what
separates Growmerce from every generic AI product.

## 07 — The Growmerce Brain

How Growmerce *thinks* — explained for a designer, because the interface is the Brain made visible.

**It reasons like a commercially-scarred operator, not like generic AI.** It thinks in systems and
evidence, separates revenue from profit reflexively, looks at the vital few things that matter (not
200 metrics), resolves symptoms to root causes, and always asks "what would make me wrong?" before
concluding.

**How it reaches conclusions:** it senses signals, grounds them in evidence, recognises patterns
against its knowledge, weighs how they interact, scores its confidence, sizes the value or threat,
decides on a prioritised action, and guards the output against error — then learns from what happens.

**How it evaluates confidence:** four honest bands — **Low / Medium / High / Very High** — *computed*
from the strength and agreement of evidence and the quality of its sources, never asserted. It is
deliberately *asymmetric*: hard to become confident, easy to lose confidence. Stronger claims require
stronger, converging, higher-quality evidence.

**How it handles uncertainty:** it says so. "We strongly suspect, and here's how we'd confirm it." It
is allowed — and required — to **abstain**: "we can't determine this yet, here's what we'd need." For
a sceptical operator, admitted uncertainty *increases* trust.

**How it avoids hallucinations:** it reasons only over *real, retrieved evidence and knowledge* — never
invented facts. Every claim traces to a source. The AI is used to read, draft, and propose, but never
to assert something with no evidence behind it. If there's no basis, it abstains rather than guessing.

**How it differs from generic AI:** generic AI is fluent and confidently wrong; it guesses, can't show
its basis, and treats every input equally. Growmerce is grounded, evidence-tiered, confidence-
calibrated, and honest about its limits.

**Designer translation:** the Brain should *feel* like a calm, brilliant, honest expert thinking out
loud — showing its reasoning and its certainty, comfortable saying "I'm not sure yet," never
performing false confidence or "AI magic." Calm competence, never spectacle.

## 08 — The Intelligence Supply Chain

Growmerce produces trusted intelligence the way a serious institution produces trusted data: through a
disciplined **supply chain**, not a single AI step. A designer should understand it because *what you
communicate at each stage builds trust.*

```
SIGNAL → EVIDENCE → PATTERN → REASONING → RECOMMENDATION → DECISION → ACTION → LEARNING
```

| Stage | What happens | What design should communicate at this stage |
|---|---|---|
| **Signal** | a raw observation is captured | "we're looking at your real situation" — humble, specific intake |
| **Evidence** | the signal is verified and rated by source quality | "this is proven, and here's where it came from" — proof + source |
| **Pattern** | a recurring reality is recognised | "this is your exact problem" — the recognition moment |
| **Reasoning** | evidence + knowledge are connected into a finding | "here's how we know" — visible, honest thinking |
| **Recommendation** | a prioritised, validated action is chosen | "here's what to do first, and why" — decisive clarity |
| **Decision** | the operator decides, informed | "you're in control, with the full basis" — empowerment |
| **Action** | Growmerce operates the fix | "we do the work" — operational competence |
| **Learning** | the outcome makes the system smarter | "we get better every time" — a compounding relationship |

The crucial design point: **Growmerce runs the whole chain, with inspection at every step** — that is
why it can be trusted, and why it is not a dashboard (which stops at "Evidence") or an AI wrapper
(which jumps from "Signal" to "Recommendation" and skips the trustworthy middle). The interface should
let the user *feel the rigour of the middle* — verification, reasoning, confidence — not just the
answer.

## 09 — The Commerce Knowledge System

**What Growmerce knows:** a structured, deep body of commercial understanding — how marketplaces,
pricing, promotion, customer behaviour, occasions, competition, and operations actually work. This is
*institutional expertise*, organised as doctrine, not opinions.

**Why it knows more:** because it captures the *outcomes* of the fixes it operates (did it work?),
organises knowledge so it transfers across businesses and channels, and validates it over time. Most
competitors' knowledge is personal (lives in people, walks out the door) and doesn't compound.

**Why knowledge compounds:** every engagement adds validated, transferable knowledge, so Growmerce
understands more every year — and a lesson learned for one business improves recommendations for all.

**Why it becomes a moat:** software, prompts, and dashboards are copyable; a validated, compounding,
transferable body of commercial understanding fed by exclusive outcome data is not. The longer
Growmerce operates, the wider the gap.

**Designer translation — interaction & visual opportunities:**
- **Recognition** is knowledge made visible: the "that's exactly my business" moment is Growmerce's
  knowledge meeting the user's reality. Make recognition cards a signature element.
- **Benchmarks** are knowledge interpreting a number: when you show a metric, show it *against what's
  normal* ("low for your category"), never as a bare figure.
- **The ecosystem map** is knowledge of the whole commerce surface: show channels/marketplaces as a
  *map Growmerce understands*, never a logo wall.
- **"It gets smarter"** is a relationship metaphor: returning users should see new intelligence,
  conveying a system that learns about *their* business over time.

Visual metaphor for knowledge: **a deep, structured, connected understanding** — calm, organised,
precise — not a "brain" icon and not a content library.

## 10 — Trust Architecture

**The most important section. Trust is the conversion engine — not marketing, not branding, not
persuasion. Trust.** A sceptical operator converts when they *trust*, and they trust when Growmerce
*demonstrates* rather than claims. Every design decision should be evaluated by: *does this build or
spend trust?*

**How trust is earned:** by demonstration, not assertion. The user must *see* Growmerce reason about
their business — name their exact problem, show the evidence, state its confidence honestly — before
being asked for anything. Show, don't claim.

**How confidence is communicated:** as a calm, honest, four-band scale (Low/Medium/High/Very High)
attached to every conclusion, always paired with *why.* It must read as *calibration*, never as
red-amber-green "good/bad." High and low confidence look different, openly. Confidence can visibly
*rise* as the user adds data — a signature trust moment.

**How evidence is communicated:** every claim carries its basis — the signals behind it, where they
came from, how fresh. Evidence is a visible design element (a panel, a proof layer), available on
demand. Never a bare number; never "trust us."

**How uncertainty is communicated:** openly and without apology. Missing data is shown as a state, not
hidden. "We can't assess this yet — here's what would let us." This honesty is a *feature*, the
strongest trust signal of all, because con artists are always certain and operators are calibrated.

**How honesty is communicated:** by what Growmerce volunteers — naming what's *not* wrong (ruled-out
findings), separating profit from revenue (the operator's tell), and admitting the edges of what it
knows. The absence of hype and manipulation is itself the signal.

**How recommendations are justified:** every recommendation traces to a finding (the problem it
solves), the evidence behind it, and its confidence. It is framed by leverage, never guaranteed.
Medium-confidence recommendations are framed as "a cheap test that also confirms," not commands.

**The three sharpest trust tools** (use them prominently — competitors won't):
1. **Honest confidence** ("we strongly suspect, here's how we'd confirm") — disarms the sceptic.
2. **Ruled-out findings** ("your discovery is fine; the leak is at checkout") — proves it's specific.
3. **Profit ≠ revenue** ("sales are up, but profit isn't") — proves an operator made it.

> Design implication: the **trust & confidence visual language is a core system, not a detail.** It is
> the single biggest differentiator and the actual conversion mechanism. Build it with the same care
> as the logo.

## 11 — Visual Philosophy

Translate everything above into how it *looks*. The direction is **Growmerce Commerce Intelligence
OS**: a calm, premium, Arabic-first commerce intelligence operating system. Inspiration (reference for
*qualities*, never to copy): Apple Spotlight, Raycast, Linear, Bloomberg Intelligence, Arc, Notion AI.

The visual principles (each derives from the strategy):

- **Calm intelligence.** Sophistication shown through *structure, precision, evidence, and restraint* —
  never through density, darkness, or noise. Confident enough to be quiet. (Resolves the core tension:
  intelligent *and* calm → calm wins.)
- **Executive confidence.** It should feel like a serious decision instrument for serious businesses —
  premium, composed, the calm of someone who knows.
- **Evidence before claims.** Proof and provenance are visible design elements; nothing is a bare
  assertion.
- **Precision before decoration.** Order, alignment, and clear hierarchy convey a reasoning mind;
  nothing is arbitrary or ornamental.
- **Intelligence before marketing.** Demonstrate understanding; never persuade with agency tropes.
- **Clarity before excitement.** Calm conviction, not hype energy. No exclamation-mark feeling.
- **Depth before density.** Rich understanding available on demand (progressive disclosure), but never
  dumped as a wall of data.
- **Honesty made visible.** Confidence, uncertainty, and ruled-out items have a first-class visual
  language.
- **Arabic-first, RTL-native, premium.** A serious stance for an under-served market — not a
  translation afterthought. The crafted Arabic wordmark (جرومرس) and typography carry this.
- **Mostly light, generous space.** Primarily light and calm; not a dark dashboard. Space *is* the
  brand — the confidence to leave it empty.
- **Mobile-first.** The default user is on a phone, often from TikTok; premium and calm must survive at
  small size, and speed is part of the identity.

Personality the visuals must embody (the narrative voice, made visual): **analytical, evidence-based,
calm, direct, quietly confident, intellectually honest, operator-grade.** Never hyped, salesy,
buzzword-y, cute, robotic, or flashy.

## 12 — Interaction Philosophy

How the interface should *behave.* Interaction is where "intelligent" and "calm" are won or lost.

- **Command surfaces.** The signature interaction is an intelligent command/console (Spotlight/Raycast
  lineage) where the user begins by asking the system to look at their business — not a hero banner
  with a form bolted on. The system feels like an instrument an operator *operates.*
- **Structured input that feels like intelligence, not paperwork.** Suggestions the user can
  accept/reject/edit/add; editable chips; honest file/URL states; a confidence indicator that rises
  with input. Apple Spotlight, never Google Forms / Typeform.
- **Progressive disclosure.** Show only what the current state needs; reveal more as certainty grows.
  Depth on demand — never overload. This is how the interface stays calm *and* deep.
- **Evidence reveal.** Proof expands gracefully when the user wants to see *how we know* — available,
  not forced.
- **Reasoning reveal.** When the system is working, it narrates *real* reasoning steps ("checking
  discovery vs. demand… separating revenue from profit…"), not a fake spinner. Honest "thinking."
- **Confidence reveal.** Confidence is visible and *changes* as evidence accrues — a signature moment
  worth designing carefully.
- **Recommendation reveal.** The prioritised plan unfolds decisively — "if you do one thing," then the
  sequence — with each item's basis available.

Motion principles: **subtle, purposeful, confidence-building, state-driven — never decorative, never
AI-magic theatre.** The interface moves like a thoughtful operator works: deliberately, quietly, and
always for a reason. Responsive and fast; latency kills the feeling of intelligence. Every interaction
either reveals intelligence or reduces friction; if it does neither, remove it.

## 13 — Homepage Philosophy

(Do not design it here — understand what it must *accomplish.*)

The homepage is the **Commerce Command Center.** Within roughly three seconds it must communicate:
Growmerce increases online sales; it understands the whole commerce ecosystem (marketplaces, stores,
delivery, social, WhatsApp); it is more intelligent and operational than an agency; and you can start
by diagnosing your own sales leaks.

**The questions it must answer (for a skeptical, scanning operator):**
- "Is this for *my* kind of business?" → yes, instantly, through specificity.
- "Do these people actually understand online sales?" → demonstrated, not claimed.
- "What is this, exactly?" → an intelligence system that finds and fixes leaks, not an agency/tool.
- "What do I do next?" → diagnose my sales leaks (one obvious action).

**The beliefs it must change:** from *"another agency/tool/AI"* to *"these people get it, and this is
different."* It changes belief by **recognition** (naming a leak the operator recognises) and a **taste
of real intelligence** (one worked finding with evidence and honest confidence), not by claims.

**The trust it must establish:** enough to start the diagnostic or open WhatsApp — earned by
demonstrated judgement and operator language, with zero hype.

**What it must not be:** a generic hero + logo wall + services grid + testimonials. One dominant action
(Diagnose), WhatsApp persistent, recognition-led, calm.

## 14 — Diagnostic Philosophy

**What the diagnostic is:** the Growmerce Brain reasoning on the user's own business, in public — the
single most important proof of expertise and the primary conversion surface. It is the intelligence,
experienced first-hand, for free.

**What it is not:** a form, a survey, a quiz, or a lead-gen funnel in disguise. No "Step 3 of 8," no
"required fields," no personality-test gimmicks, no email wall before value.

**Why it exists:** to let the user *feel* Growmerce's judgement before any commitment — turning
skepticism into trust by demonstration, and producing a real, warm lead with a genuine diagnosis.

**How users should feel during it:** like a sharp commerce operator is interviewing them and visibly
*using* what they say — intelligent, effortless, honest. Each step builds on the last. It should feel
like watching a real intelligence narrow toward their specific problem, and wanting more of it.

**How it differs from forms and surveys:** a form extracts; the diagnostic *reasons.* It shows its
work, reveals what it's drawing on and what it's missing, offers suggestions to accept/reject/edit,
delivers value before asking for anything, and is honest about confidence and gaps. Every step is
skippable; missing input lowers confidence rather than blocking. It never fabricates a finding to
fill a gap.

The payload is the **executive decision brief** (the report): decision-first, evidence-backed,
confidence-scored, prioritised — never a dashboard or a PDF.

## 15 — Intelligence OS Philosophy

Growmerce uses an **operating-system metaphor**: a calm command center for online sales, an instrument
an operator runs — not a dashboard they stare at.

**When the OS metaphor should appear:** the command/input surface; the structured-input console; the
way evidence, reasoning, confidence, and recommendations are surfaced as a coherent system; the
ecosystem map as a live intelligence surface; the report as an intelligence terminal's output rendered
as a calm executive brief.

**When it should NOT appear:** it must never tip into a *dashboard* (dense metric walls, chart grids),
a *sci-fi/futuristic UI* (fake-futuristic decoration), or a *developer-tool clone* (literal IDE look).
The OS feeling is **calm command and structured intelligence**, borrowed from Spotlight/Linear's calm,
not from Bloomberg-terminal density or sci-fi spectacle.

**How it differs from a dashboard:** a dashboard *displays data and stops*, leaving the thinking to the
user. The Growmerce OS *reasons and concludes* — it shows interpreted evidence, confidence, and a
prioritised decision. It is the difference between raw material and finished intelligence. Visually:
primarily light and spacious (not dark and dense); decision-first (not metric-first); interpreted (not
raw).

## 16 — Must-Have Experiences (ranked)

The experiences Claude Design must create, in priority order, with why each matters:

   ("your discount lifts orders but erases margin"). *This is the entire promise made tangible* — the

   ruled-out items, and risk are shown — calm, honest, reasoning-linked. *This is the conversion

   confidence-scored, profit-vs-revenue separated. *The payload where trust converts to action.*
4. **The Command / Structured Input.** The Spotlight/Raycast-style intelligent console with
   suggestions and chips. *The first tactile proof that this is real intelligence, not a form.*
5. **The Diagnostic Flow.** The Brain reasoning in public, state by state. *The primary proof of
   expertise.*
6. **The Commerce Ecosystem Map.** Channels/marketplaces as a map Growmerce understands (never a logo
   wall). *Demonstrates whole-ecosystem fluency.*
7. **The Mobile / TikTok Landing Experience.** Fast, calm, hook-honoring, one-tap to WhatsApp. *Most
   users arrive here first.*
8. **The Bilingual Wordmark & Arabic-first System.** Crafted جرومرس + premium Arabic typography. *The
   most-seen element; proves the Arabic-first stance.*
9. **The Return / Living-Intelligence Surface.** Saved profile, opportunity tracker, "what changed."
   *Turns a tool into a platform you come back to.*
10. **The WhatsApp / Consultation Handoff.** Context-aware, low-pressure, peer-to-peer. *Where
    commitment becomes a relationship.*

## 17 — Must-Never-Do List (the blacklist)

Explicit traps. If any appears, the work is wrong by default.

**Never look like:**
- Crypto / Web3 (gradients, hype, speculation aesthetics).
- An AI toy (neon, glowing orbs, sparkles, "magic," robot or brain imagery).
- A SaaS dashboard (dark metric walls, chart grids, data overload).
- A marketing agency (hero photography, gradient hype, "we grow brands," logo walls, portfolio walls).
- A generic startup (trend-chasing visuals, buzzword decoration).
- A growth-hacker product (loud, hype-driven, "explode your sales" energy).
- A BI/analytics tool (pivot tables, query builders, "explore your data").
- A consulting deck (framework diagrams, stiff corporate visuals).

**Never use (visual):** neon, cheap loud green, alarmist red theatre, generic SaaS blue, crypto
gradients, dark-only identity, robot/brain/AI-sparkle iconography, growth-arrow/shopping-cart/generic-G
logo clichés, stock business photography, platform logo walls, fake-futuristic/sci-fi UI.

**Never do (interaction):** fake spinners or artificial loading delays; decorative/looping animation;
bouncy or playful motion; AI-magic effects; manipulative urgency (countdowns, "limited slots," fake
scarcity); email walls before value; long forms / "Step X of Y"; dark patterns of any kind.

**Never communicate (tone, even visually):** hype; fake certainty; guarantees of outcomes; "AI knows";
vanity over substance; revenue celebrated without profit; talking down to the operator.

> The unifying test: if it could appear on an agency site, an AI-startup landing page, a crypto
> project, or a SaaS dashboard, it is wrong. Growmerce looks like a calm, premium, Arabic-first
> intelligence instrument an expert operator trusts.

## 18 — Design North Star

If Claude Design remembers only one thing:

> **Make Growmerce feel like a calm, premium, Arabic-first commerce intelligence operating system that
> a sceptical, serious operator trusts on sight — because it demonstrates that it understands their
> online sales better than they do, shows its evidence, and is honest about how sure it is. Build
> trust by demonstration, not persuasion. Calm intelligence over hype. Evidence before claims.
> Profit, not vanity. The feeling to produce, always, is: "This is not an agency. This is a commerce
> intelligence operating system — and it gets my business."**

Everything else in this brief is in service of that sentence.
