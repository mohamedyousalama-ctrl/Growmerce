# Mobile-First Brand Experience

> The identity must perform on a phone first. The default Growmerce visitor arrives on mobile —
> often from a TikTok video — scanning fast, in Arabic, with seconds of patience. The brand's
> premium, calm, intelligent feel must survive (and thrive) on a small screen. If the identity only
> works on desktop, it doesn't work.

This implements `04-website-ux/Mobile_TikTok_Landing_Experience.md` in identity terms.

---

## 1. Why mobile-first is non-negotiable

- TikTok is a primary acquisition channel (12); that traffic is mobile, vertical, impatient.
- The Arabic-first audience reads on phones.
- WhatsApp — the primary conversation channel — is mobile-native.

So the identity is **designed for mobile first**, then scaled up — not designed for desktop and
shrunk. Every signature element (command surface, recognition card, report, confidence language,
ecosystem map) must have a considered mobile form.

---

## 2. What the mobile identity must deliver

| Need | Identity requirement |
|---|---|
| **TikTok landing speed** | instant, calm, recognisable insight within a few thumb-scrolls |
| **Small screens** | premium feel preserved at phone size; nothing cramped |
| **Fast scanning** | one clear focal point per screen; glanceable hierarchy |
| **Arabic reading** | flawless small-size Arabic legibility, RTL-native |
| **Quick pattern recognition** | recognition cards instantly legible on mobile |
| **Diagnostic entry** | one-tap, low-friction start; command surface adapted to touch |
| **WhatsApp CTA** | sticky, one-thumb-tap, always reachable |

---

## 3. Mobile design principles

- **One thing per screen.** Calm, single-focus views; progressive disclosure does even more work on
  mobile (`Layout_and_Spatial_Principles.md`).
- **Thumb-first.** Primary actions in reach; tap/paste/swipe over typing.
- **Premium at small size.** Space, type, and restraint preserve the premium feel — resist the urge
  to cram. Calm scales down; clutter doesn't.
- **Glanceable hierarchy.** The important thing is unmistakable in a half-second scan.
- **Arabic-first legibility.** Type tuned for small Arabic; tested in real conditions (bright
  light, one hand).
- **Speed is identity.** Performance and smoothness are part of feeling premium and intelligent;
  jank or slowness breaks the brand (`Motion_and_Interaction_Principles.md`).

---

## 4. Signature elements on mobile

| Element | Mobile form |
|---|---|
| **Command surface** | a focused, touch-friendly command/input; paste a link or tap a channel; minimal typing |
| **Recognition card** | full-width, instantly legible; the pattern words are the hero |
| **Report** | headline + top finding above the fold; findings as expandable cards; impact as clear ranges; sticky next step |
| **Confidence language** | confidence markers legible and meaningful at small size, calm not alarmist |
| **Ecosystem map** | collapses to a recognisable, swipeable channel strip/grouping, not a dense graph |
| **Diagnostic** | one calm step per screen; honest reasoning states; skippable everywhere |

---

## 5. The TikTok-landing moment

The fastest, highest-velocity entry (`04-website-ux/Mobile_TikTok_Landing_Experience.md`):
- The lander **honours the video's hook** instantly (continuity) — the first screen matches the
  video.
- A recognition + a real, calm insight within a few scrolls.
- One-tap into the diagnostic or WhatsApp.
- Ruthless friction budget: no pop-ups, no walls, minimal load weight — speed *is* the brand here.

The identity must look premium and trustworthy even in this fast, stripped-down context — calm
confidence at speed.

---

## 6. What to avoid on mobile

- Desktop layouts squeezed down (cramped, broken hierarchy).
- Dense dashboards or data walls on a phone.
- Tiny or poorly-shaped Arabic text.
- Heavy assets that slow load (speed is identity).
- Pop-ups, interstitials, or friction on the first screen.
- Losing the premium/calm feel "because it's mobile" — restraint scales, clutter doesn't.

---

## 7. Deliverables for Claude Design (mobile)

- Mobile-first forms of every signature element (command surface, recognition card, report,
  confidence language, ecosystem map, diagnostic).
- The TikTok-lander identity (fast, calm, hook-honoring, one-tap).
- Arabic small-size legibility proof in real conditions.
- Performance/restraint guidance (mobile speed as identity).

---

## 8. The test

On a phone, from a cold TikTok visit, does Growmerce feel **premium, calm, intelligent, and
trustworthy** — and can the visitor recognise their problem and act in a few thumb-scrolls? If the
identity only feels right on a desktop, it has failed its most important context. Mobile-first is
where the brand meets most of its market.
