# Trust & Confidence Visual Language

> A core differentiator. Growmerce's honesty — showing evidence, calibrating confidence, admitting
> what it doesn't know — is verbal in the narrative (21) and computed in the Brain (20). This
> document makes it **visual.** How Growmerce *shows* evidence strength, confidence, assumptions,
> missing data, risk, priority, and human review is a competitive moat that generic AI/agency
> brands cannot easily copy, because they have no real machine behind it.

---

## 1. Why this is central, not peripheral

Most brands treat trust as testimonials and badges. Growmerce treats trust as a **visible property
of every conclusion.** The Trust & Confidence visual language is therefore a core system, used
everywhere findings appear — diagnostics, reports, recommendations, the homepage proof, content. If
this language is strong, the brand is unmistakably "an evidence-driven intelligence system"; if
it's weak, Growmerce looks like every other AI audit tool.

---

| Element | What it shows | Source |

Each needs a consistent, calm visual treatment that reads the same everywhere.

---

## 3. The confidence visual language

The signature element. Principles:
- A **calm, legible scale** for the four bands — distinguishable at a glance, **without** red-amber-
  green "good/bad" alarmism. Confidence is about *certainty*, not *good news* — the visual must not
  conflate them.
- **Honest, not flattering** — Medium and Low confidence are shown as openly as High. The system is
  comfortable saying "we're not sure yet."
- **Can change** — confidence visibly updates as evidence is added (a signature motion,
  `Motion_and_Interaction_Principles.md`). Watching confidence rise as you give more data is a
  trust-building moment.
- **Always paired with reasoning** — the confidence marker links to *why* (the evidence), never a
  bare score (mirrors the narrative grammar: claim + evidence + confidence).
- Colour from the **confidence/evidence role** in `Color_Direction.md`.

---

## 4. The evidence visual language

- **Evidence panels** show the proof behind a claim on demand — sources, signals, tier — without
  cluttering the primary view (progressive disclosure).
- **Tiered treatment**: hard data, derived signal, and self-reported/anecdote are visually
  distinguishable (so the viewer can see *what kind* of evidence supports a claim).
- **Provenance is a design element** — the chain from signal → conclusion can be revealed; the
  intelligence shows its work.

---

## 5. Honesty states (the differentiator made visual)

Generic tools only show findings and certainty. Growmerce's visual system has first-class states
for **honesty**:

- **Missing data:** a calm, non-alarming state — "we can't assess this yet; here's what would let
  us" — never a blank or a hidden gap.
- **Assumptions:** visibly flagged where a finding rests on one.
- **Ruled-out / what's healthy:** a treatment for naming what's *not* wrong (credibility through
  balance) — reports and diagnostics need a place for this.
- **Verification step:** "here's how we'd confirm this" shown as a clear next action, turning
  uncertainty into a path.

These states are what make the design *honest by construction* — and they're hard to fake without
a real Brain behind them.

---

## 6. Risk & priority visual language

- **Risk:** shown **calmly** — serious and legible, never loud-red theatre. Risk is communicated
  like an operator stating a real exposure, not a fire alarm (personality: calm).
- **Priority:** the "do this first" is visually unmistakable — order, weight, and position all
  reinforce sequence (`Layout_and_Spatial_Principles.md`, action-priority surfaces).
- **Profit vs. revenue:** impact distinguishes the two visually (the H1 distinction, also in
  `Color_Direction.md`).

---

## 7. Human review (trust signal)

Where a human operator has verified or signed off a high-stakes finding (Human Override, 20), a
**quiet "reviewed by a commerce specialist" treatment** signals the human-plus-machine trust model
— without theatrics. This is a genuine differentiator: the intelligence is supervised, and the
design says so calmly.

---

## 8. Principles across the whole trust language

- **Calm, not alarmist** — certainty, risk, and priority are shown with operator composure.
- **Honest by default** — uncertainty and gaps are first-class, not hidden.
- **Consistent** — the same element means the same thing everywhere; learnable and trustworthy.
- **Always tied to reasoning** — no bare scores; confidence/evidence link to *why*.
- **Restrained** — these are precise instruments, not colourful badges.

---

## 9. What to avoid

- Red-amber-green "good/bad" coding of confidence (conflates certainty with sentiment).
- Bare confidence scores with no evidence link.
- Hiding missing data or assumptions to look more certain (violates the whole premise).
- Alarmist risk visuals (loud red, warning theatre).
- Trust "badges"/testimonials substituting for visible evidence.

---

## 10. Deliverables for Claude Design (trust/confidence)

- The **confidence visual language** (four bands, calm, change-capable, reasoning-linked).
- The **evidence visual language** (panels, tiers, provenance).
- **Honesty states**: missing data, assumptions, ruled-out, verification step.
- **Risk** and **priority** visual treatments (calm; profit-vs-revenue distinct).
- The **human-review** trust treatment.

> This visual language is where Growmerce's defining claim — *we tell you how sure we are* — becomes
> something you can *see*. Get it right and the brand is visibly, structurally trustworthy in a way
> no logo-wall agency or certainty-faking AI tool can match.
