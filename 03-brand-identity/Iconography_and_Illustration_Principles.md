# Iconography & Illustration Principles

> What Growmerce draws — and what it refuses to draw. Iconography and illustration are where
> "intelligence" brands most often slip into cliché (brains, robots, glowing nodes). Growmerce's
> visual language is **systems, signals, and commerce flows** — diagrams an operator recognises,
> not decorative AI imagery.

---

## 1. The governing principle

> Growmerce illustrates **how commerce actually works** — flows, signals, patterns, channels,
> confidence — as clean, functional, system-grade visuals. It never illustrates *abstract
> "AI/tech"* (brains, robots, circuits, glowing orbs). The imagery is operational, not decorative.

Illustration here is closer to **intelligent diagramming** than to spot illustration. It teaches
and reveals; it doesn't ornament.

---

## 2. What to avoid (hard no's)

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| **Generic AI icons** (sparkles, glowing orbs) | shallow "AI startup" signal |
| **Robots** | literal, untrustworthy AI cliché |
| **Brains** | the laziest "intelligence" cliché |
| **Generic dashboards** (as illustration) | "SaaS vendor"; cognitive noise |
| **Shopping carts / bags** | shallow ecommerce literalism |
| **Random 3D objects / blobs** | trend-following decoration, no meaning |
| **Stock business photography** | agency aesthetic; generic |
| **Circuit-board / "digital" textures** | tech cliché; not what we are |

If an icon or illustration could appear on any AI/SaaS/agency site, it's wrong.

---

## 3. What to use (the Growmerce visual vocabulary)

| Prefer | What it expresses |
|---|---|
| **System diagrams** | the intelligence pipeline, flows, how parts connect (operational) |
| **Signal maps** | sensing, evidence, signal strength (intelligence/evidence) |
| **Commerce flows** | the path from demand → discovery → conversion → profit (operations) |
| **Confidence markers** | calibrated certainty as a clean visual element (trust) |
| **Pattern nodes** | recurring patterns, recognition, connections (the Pattern Engine, 19) |
| **Channel maps** | the commerce ecosystem as a network (marketplace knowledge) |
| **Marketplace / ecosystem maps** | platforms as an operating layer (`Platform_Ecosystem_Visualization.md`) |

These form a coherent family: **a calm, precise, diagrammatic language for commerce intelligence.**

---

## 4. Icon style principles

- **Functional and precise**, not decorative — icons label and clarify, they don't entertain.
- **Consistent geometry and weight**; a disciplined, system-grade set.
- **Calm and restrained** — line-based or quietly filled; never glowing, neon, or 3D-flashy.
- **Meaningful, not literal** — express the concept (signal, flow, pattern), not a literal object
  (cart, bag, robot).
- **RTL-aware** — directional icons respect Arabic reading direction.
- **Legible at small sizes** and on mobile.

---

## 5. Illustration as intelligent diagramming

The "illustration" Growmerce uses is mostly **diagrammatic visualisation of its own intelligence**:
- The cognition pipeline (SENSE → … → LEARN) as a clean system diagram (`Intelligence_OS_Visual_Metaphor.md`).
- Evidence → confidence → finding as a visible chain.
- Patterns and their interactions as nodes and connections.
- Channels/marketplaces as an ecosystem network.

This doubles as *content*: these diagrams explain how Growmerce thinks, which is itself proof of
expertise (Trust & Proof, 04/21). The illustration *is* the argument.

---

## 6. Restraint and meaning

- Every visual element should **mean something** — represent a real concept from the strategy. No
  decorative imagery.
- **Less is premium.** A few precise diagrams beat many busy illustrations.
- Imagery supports comprehension and trust; it never competes with the calm or adds noise.

---

## 7. Photography (if any)

- Default to **little or no stock photography** — it reads as agency/generic.
- If real imagery is used, prefer authentic operational context (real commerce, real products,
  real screens) over staged business stock.
- Never use AI-generated "futuristic" imagery or robot/abstract-tech visuals.

---

## 8. Deliverables for Claude Design (icon/illustration)

- A **system-grade icon set** in the Growmerce vocabulary (signals, flows, patterns, confidence,
  channels), RTL-aware, mobile-legible.
- A **diagrammatic illustration style** for visualising the intelligence (pipeline, evidence,
  patterns, ecosystem).
- Clear do/don't examples encoding this document's hard no's and preferred vocabulary.

---

## 9. The test

Does the icon/illustration look like a **precise diagram of how commerce or intelligence works** —
something an operator reads and understands — rather than decorative "AI/tech" imagery? If it's a
brain, a robot, a sparkle, a cart, or a glowing abstract object, it's the wrong language. Growmerce
draws systems, not symbols of "smart."
