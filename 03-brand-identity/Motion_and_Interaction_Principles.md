# Motion & Interaction Principles

> How Growmerce moves. Motion is where "intelligent" and "calm" are either reinforced or betrayed.
> Growmerce's motion is **subtle, purposeful, and confidence-building** — it expresses the system
> thinking and responding, never decoration or spectacle.

---

## 1. The governing principle

> Motion exists to communicate **state and reasoning** — the system sensing, considering,
> responding, becoming more certain. Every animation has a job: to make a transition legible, to
> reveal evidence, to show confidence changing, to confirm an action. If a motion doesn't carry
> meaning, it doesn't ship.

This mirrors the personality (21): calm, analytical, evidence-based. The interface moves the way a
thoughtful operator works — deliberately, not theatrically.

---

## 2. Motion principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Subtle** | quiet, restrained; felt more than noticed |
| **Purposeful** | every motion communicates state or guides attention |
| **Confidence-building** | smooth, stable motion signals a system in control |
| **State-transition driven** | motion expresses moving between user states / data states |
| **Not decorative** | no animation for spectacle; no looping ornament |
| **Fast and calm** | responsive, never sluggish; never frantic |
| **RTL-aware** | directional motion respects Arabic reading direction |

---

## 3. What motion must support

Motion is tied to the real interactions of the intelligence system (04/20):

| Interaction | Motion's job |
|---|---|
| **Search / command** | the command surface feels instant, responsive, Spotlight-smooth |
| **Selection** | clear, calm feedback on choosing/focusing |
| **Accept / reject / edit** (suggestions) | satisfying, legible confirmation of the human-in-the-loop action |
| **Diagnosis progress** | honest sense of real reasoning happening (not a fake spinner) |
| **Evidence reveal** | proof/detail expands calmly on demand (progressive disclosure) |
| **Confidence change** | confidence visibly updating as evidence is added — a signature moment |
| **Recommendation priority** | priority/sequence becoming clear through ordered motion |

The **confidence-change** and **evidence-reveal** motions are signature: they make the trust
language *move*, turning calibrated honesty into a felt experience.

---

## 4. The "system thinking" motion (handled honestly)

When the Brain is working (diagnosis progress), motion must:
- Convey **genuine reasoning steps**, not a decorative loading spinner
  (`04-website-ux/Commerce_Diagnostic_Experience.md`).
- Be **brief and calm** — never artificial delay for drama.
- Reinforce trust: it should feel like watching a competent system consider, not like a
  progress-bar gimmick.

This is the one place "AI thinking" is shown — and it must be shown as *honest work*, not magic
(guardrails: no AI-magic theatre).

---

## 5. Interaction principles

- **Responsive and immediate.** The command/console surface reacts instantly; latency is the enemy
  of "intelligent."
- **Calm feedback.** Interactions confirm quietly and clearly — no bouncy, playful, or flashy
  reactions.
- **Progressive, not abrupt.** Disclosure and transitions are smooth, matching the layout's
  progressive-disclosure logic.
- **Forgiving and reversible.** Accept/reject/edit, undo, easy exits — respecting the operator
  (personality: respectful).
- **Consistent.** The same interaction means the same thing everywhere; a learnable, trustworthy
  system.

---

## 6. What to avoid

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| **Decorative/looping animation** | spectacle; contradicts calm and purpose |
| **Bouncy, playful motion** | undercuts seriousness/premium |
| **Flashy transitions / parallax theatre** | "marketing site"; noise |
| **Fake AI-magic effects** (glows, particles, "thinking" sparkles) | hype; violates guardrails |
| **Slow, heavy animation** | feels sluggish; undercuts "intelligent" |
| **Artificial loading delays** | dishonest; breaks trust |
| **Motion with no meaning** | fails the purpose test |

---

## 7. Performance & restraint

- Motion must be **smooth on mobile** (the default device) and never compromise speed —
  performance *is* part of feeling premium and intelligent.
- **Restraint:** a small, consistent motion vocabulary used precisely, not a showcase of effects.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences (accessibility + calm).

---

## 8. Deliverables for Claude Design (motion)

- A **motion vocabulary**: defined behaviours for command/search, selection, accept/reject/edit,
  diagnosis progress, evidence reveal, confidence change, and priority.
- Timing/easing principles that read as calm-and-responsive (not bouncy, not slow).
- The **confidence-change** and **honest reasoning-progress** motions specified as signature
  moments.
- RTL and reduced-motion handling.

---

## 9. The test

Does the motion make the system feel **calm, responsive, and genuinely thinking** — communicating
state and building confidence — or does it feel like decoration, spectacle, or AI-magic theatre? If
a motion could be removed without losing meaning, remove it. Growmerce moves like a competent
operator: deliberately, quietly, and always for a reason.
