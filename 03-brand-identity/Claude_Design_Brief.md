# Claude Design Brief

> **The handoff document.** Everything Claude Design needs to begin visual exploration of the
> Growmerce identity, in one place. The detailed reasoning lives in the other documents of this
> workstream; this is the executive brief that points to them and sets the bar.

---

## 1. The assignment

Create the visual identity system for **Growmerce (جرومرس)** — an **Online Sales Growth
Operations** company powered by **Commerce Intelligence**. Give visual form to a company whose
intelligence (20), experience (04), and voice (21) are already fully defined. The identity must
make a serious operator feel **"this company understands online sales deeply,"** never **"this is a
nice-looking agency."**

This is visual exploration: logo directions, type, colour, layout, iconography, motion, and the
intelligence visual systems. Not a final locked system on day one — directions to evaluate against
the criteria below.

---

## 2. Brand positioning (north star)

> **Growmerce is Online Sales Growth Operations, powered by Commerce Intelligence.** It finds where
> a business's online sales and profit leak — across marketplaces, ecommerce, delivery apps, social
> commerce, and WhatsApp — with evidence and honest confidence, and **operates the fixes.**

- **Is not:** a marketing agency, generic consultancy, SaaS dashboard, content company, or shallow
  AI tool.
- **Serves:** serious, sceptical, busy commerce operators; Arabic-first market.
- **Promise:** find the leak (revenue *and* profit), prove it, operate the fix.
- **Feeling:** "these people understand online sales deeply." Calm, premium, trustworthy.

(Full: `Brand_Positioning_Summary.md`.)

---

## 3. The narrative (the voice the visuals must match)

Growmerce speaks like a **commercially-scarred, evidence-driven, intellectually-honest commerce
operator** — analytical, calm, direct, quietly confident; never hyped, salesy, or buzzword-heavy
(`21-brand-narrative-system`). **The identity must look the way the narrative sounds.** Calm
intelligence, visible evidence, honest confidence, no hype.

---

## 4. The UX state model (what the visuals dress)

The website is organised around **user states**, not pages (`04-website-ux/User_State_Journey.md`):

> Curiosity → Recognition → Diagnosis → Evidence → Trust → Action → Commitment → Return

Plus the retention model that turns visitors into recurring intelligence users. The identity must
support this journey of *progressive certainty* — and the signature surfaces: the **command-center
input**, **recognition cards**, the **executive report**, and the **trust/confidence** language.

---

## 5. Selected visual direction

**Direction A — Commerce Intelligence**, evolved into:

> **Growmerce Commerce Intelligence OS** — a calm, premium, Arabic-first commerce intelligence
> operating system / command center for online sales.

**Inspiration (reference, never copy):** Apple Spotlight, Raycast, Linear, Bloomberg Intelligence,
Arc Browser, Notion AI. Borrow their virtues (calm command, precision, premium craft, serious
information, fluid modernity, approachable intelligence) — copy none. Growmerce is Arabic-first,
which forces an original system. (Full: `Visual_Direction_Principles.md`.)

**Must feel:** smart · premium · calm · operational · evidence-based · modern · Arabic-first ·
practical · high-trust.

## 7. Hard no's (do not cross)

- Agency aesthetics (hero photos, gradient hype, "we grow brands").
- Neon AI gradients · robot imagery · brain icons · glowing orbs/sparkles.
- Generic startup visuals · fake futuristic/sci-fi interfaces.
- Crypto/Web3 visual language.
- Dark-dashboard overload · metric walls.
- Overly corporate consulting visuals.
- Growth-arrow / shopping-cart / generic-G / ecommerce-bag logo clichés.
- Logo walls for platforms (use the ecosystem map).
- English-first design with Arabic bolted on.
- Hype, fake certainty, or AI-magic theatre in any visual form (mirrors the narrative guardrails).

---

## 8. Deliverables expected (from the design phase)

- **Logo:** explorations across the conceptual directions; a primary **bilingual lockup** (Latin +
  جرومرس) + optional standalone symbol; small-size/mono/light-dark proofs.
- **Type:** an Arabic-first system (display + functional), with specimens and a hierarchy for web,
  reports, diagnostics; bilingual + mobile proofs.
- **Colour:** the role-based semantic palette (incl. profit-vs-revenue and the confidence/evidence
  scale); light-primary, accessible, Arabic-legible.
- **Visual systems:** the Intelligence-OS metaphor, the ecosystem map, recognition cards, the
  executive report, and the trust/confidence visual language.
- **Motion & layout:** the motion vocabulary and the spatial/layout system (command surfaces,
  executive document, mobile-first).
- **Application:** how it all comes together on the homepage command center, the diagnostic, and
  the report — desktop and mobile, Arabic-first.

---

## 9. Evaluation criteria (how to judge any direction)

A direction is right when it passes all five (the positioning test, `Brand_Positioning_Summary.md`):

1. **Identity:** looks like an **operator's intelligence system**, not an agency/dashboard/AI
   startup.
2. **Feeling:** **calm and premium**, never loud or hyped.
3. **Arabic-first:** native, premium Arabic — not English translated.
4. **Intelligence visible:** evidence, confidence, and reasoning are *shown*, not decorated.
5. **Trust:** a sceptical, serious operator would **trust it on sight**.

And the meta-test: does it make people feel **"this company understands online sales deeply"**?
