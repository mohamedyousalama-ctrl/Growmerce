# Layout & Spatial Principles

> How space, structure, and hierarchy are organised. In a calm, premium, intelligence-led
> identity, **layout is where "calm" and "operational" are won or lost.** These principles govern
> every surface — homepage, command center, diagnostic, report, recognition cards, mobile.

---

## 1. The governing principles

| Principle | Meaning | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Calm whitespace** | generous space; few elements competing | personality: calm |
| **Low cognitive load** | one main thing per surface; progressive disclosure | UX (04) + calm |
| **Command surfaces** | central intelligent console, Apple/Raycast lineage | direction |
| **Executive-document clarity** | report = decision brief, not dashboard | reports (04) |
| **Structured hierarchy** | strong grid; the important is obviously primary | personality: direct/analytical |
| **RTL-native** | Arabic-first spatial logic, not flipped LTR | Arabic-first |
| **Mobile-first hierarchy** | works thumb-first, glanceable | UX (04) / TikTok |

---

## 2. Calm through space

The single most important spatial decision: **use space generously.** Calm is created by what's
*not* there. Principles:
- Few simultaneous focal points; ideally one primary action/idea per view.
- Generous margins and padding; content breathes.
- Quiet separation (space and subtle surfaces) over heavy borders/dividers.
- Resist filling space; emptiness is premium, not wasted.

This is the antidote to "dashboard overload" and "agency busy-ness." The confidence to leave space
*is* the brand.

---

## 3. Command-surface layout

The signature surface (from `Visual_Direction_Principles.md`): a central intelligent
command/console.
- The primary input is a **calm, focused command surface** (Spotlight/Raycast lineage), not a hero
  banner with a bolted-on field.
- Around it, **progressive disclosure**: the system reveals the next relevant thing in response to
  input, rather than showing everything at once (`04-website-ux/Structured_Input_Experience.md`).
- Suggestion cards, chips, and results appear *in response*, in a calm, structured way.

---

## 4. Progressive disclosure (the structural backbone)

Mirroring the user-state journey (04) and the Brain's progressive reveal (20), layouts disclose
information in step with the user's certainty:
- Show only what the current state needs; reveal more as the user advances.
- Detail (evidence, full reasoning) is available **on demand**, not dumped upfront.
- This keeps every surface calm *and* lets the system be deep — depth without overload.

---

## 5. Executive-decision-document layout (reports)

Reports are laid out as **executive decision briefs**, not dashboards (`Report_and_Diagnostic_Visual_Language.md`):
- **Decision-first:** headline diagnosis at the top, then the system narrative, then prioritised
  findings.
- **One finding = one clear unit** (a card), scannable: finding → evidence → confidence → impact →
  action.
- **Prioritised, sequenced** vertical flow (Now → Next → Plan), not a grid of equal tiles.
- **Generous, readable** long-form rhythm; room for nuance (ruled-out, missing data).
- Charts only where they serve a decision — never decoration.

---

## 6. Pattern recognition cards

The recurring content unit (`04-website-ux/Pattern_Recognition_Experience.md`) needs a defined
spatial treatment:
- A **calm card** that holds: the pattern in operator words (primary), why/how-we'd-know
  (secondary), confidence (a marker), and one action.
- Consistent structure so recognition cards are instantly legible everywhere (homepage, diagnostic,
  market pages).
- Restrained; the *words* are the hero, the card is the quiet frame.

---

## 7. Evidence panels & action-priority surfaces

- **Evidence panels:** a defined layout for showing the proof behind a claim — sources, signals,
  confidence — available on demand without cluttering the primary view.
- **Action-priority surfaces:** the prioritised recommendations laid out so "do this first" is
  spatially unmistakable (order, weight, position all reinforce priority).

These give the operational and evidence-based qualities a concrete spatial home.

---

## 8. RTL-native spatial logic

Arabic-first means the layout is **designed RTL**, not designed LTR and flipped:
- Reading order, alignment, primary-to-secondary flow, and visual weight follow RTL.
- Bilingual elements (Latin platform names, numerals) sit naturally within RTL structure.
- Iconography and directional elements respect RTL (e.g. flow/progression direction).

---

## 9. Mobile-first hierarchy

The default visitor is on a phone (`Mobile_First_Brand_Experience.md`):
- One clear thing per screen; thumb-reachable primary actions.
- Cards and panels stack into a calm vertical flow; progressive disclosure does more work on small
  screens.
- The command surface, recognition card, and report all have considered mobile forms — not
  desktop layouts squeezed down.
- Sticky, one-tap WhatsApp and "Diagnose" actions.

---

## 10. What to avoid

- Dashboard density (many panels/metrics competing).
- Equal-weight grids that bury the important thing.
- Hero-banner marketing layouts.
- Heavy borders/dividers instead of space.
- Decorative layout flourishes that add no meaning.
- LTR layouts flipped for Arabic as an afterthought.

---

## 11. The layout test

Does the surface feel **calm, structured, and decisive** — one obvious focal point, room to
breathe, the important thing clearly primary, and depth available but not dumped? If it feels busy,
flat, or dashboard-like, it has failed the calm-and-operational standard. Space and hierarchy are
where this identity proves it understands restraint.
