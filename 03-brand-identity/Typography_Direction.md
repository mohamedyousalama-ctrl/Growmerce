# Typography Direction

> Typography is the load-bearing element of this identity. Because the direction is calm,
> premium, and command-center-like — and Arabic-first — **type and space do most of the work.**
> This document defines the typographic direction and requirements. It does not pick a final font
> (that is a design-phase decision), but it constrains the choice tightly.

---

## 1. Why typography matters more than usual here

The visual direction (`Visual_Direction_Principles.md`) is intentionally restrained — few
elements, generous space, little decoration. In such a system, **typography carries the brand.**
The choice and handling of type is what will make Growmerce feel premium and intelligent (or
generic and cheap). Type is the primary craft surface.

---

## 2. Arabic-first requirement

The type system is **Arabic-first**:
- The **Arabic typeface is the primary decision**; the Latin typeface is chosen to *pair with it*,
  not the other way around. (Most brands do the reverse and bolt on a mismatched Arabic — Growmerce
  must not.)
- Arabic and Latin must feel like **one system**: matched weight, rhythm, x-height/baseline
  relationship, and tone of seriousness.
- RTL is native; the type handles Arabic shaping, ligatures, and diacritics correctly and
  beautifully.

---

## 3. What the type system must support

| Need | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Arabic Fusha** | premium, highly readable business Arabic; correct shaping |
| **Business seriousness** | a serious, credible tone — not playful, not decorative |
| **Diagnostic readability** | clear at UI sizes; comfortable for questions/answers |
| **Report readability** | excellent for long-form executive reading; clear hierarchy |
| **Metrics & numerals** | clean, unambiguous figures (Arabic-Indic and/or Western digits — decide per context); tabular figures for data |
| **English platform names** | Latin set renders Amazon, Noon, Salla, TikTok, etc. cleanly within Arabic text |
| **Bilingual UI** | Arabic + Latin coexisting in the same component without friction |
| **Mobile reading** | legible and calm on small screens at a glance |

---

## 4. Typographic style directions to explore

Three candidate directions (Claude Design explores; one or a hybrid is chosen):

### A — Modern geometric Arabic
- Clean, contemporary, slightly geometric Arabic paired with a geometric/neo-grotesque Latin.
- **Feels:** modern, clean, systematic, software-grade.
- **Risk:** can tip toward "generic startup" if too neutral — must retain warmth and craft.

### B — Premium editorial Arabic
- A refined, editorial Arabic with more contrast/character, paired with a premium Latin.
- **Feels:** premium, serious, intelligent, trustworthy — "this is for serious businesses."
- **Risk:** must stay readable at UI/mobile sizes; editorial can become decorative.

### C — Operator / intelligence hybrid
- A clear, slightly technical type system (precise, neutral, data-friendly) for UI and metrics,
  paired with a more characterful display face for headlines/wordmark.
- **Feels:** like an intelligence instrument — calm, precise, command-center.
- **Likely strongest** for the Commerce Intelligence OS direction, possibly combined with B's
  premium character in display.

**Recommended exploration:** a **two-tier system** — a *display* voice (premium/characterful, for
headlines, recognition cards, the wordmark) + a *functional* voice (precise/neutral, for UI,
metrics, reports). Both Arabic-first, both part of one family of seriousness. This serves both the
"premium" and "operational/intelligence" qualities at once.

---

## 5. Typographic principles (regardless of font chosen)

- **Hierarchy through scale and weight, not decoration.** Clear levels; the important thing is
  obviously important (personality: direct).
- **Generous line spacing and measure.** Calm reading; never cramped (personality: calm).
- **Restraint in weights/styles.** A small, disciplined set — not a dozen weights used loudly.
- **Tabular, aligned numerals for data.** Metrics must line up and read precisely (personality:
  analytical/evidence).
- **Confident, quiet display type.** Headlines premium and calm, never shouty.
- **Excellent small-size Arabic.** The mobile/TikTok audience reads Arabic on small screens — this
  must be flawless.

---

## 6. What to avoid

- A Latin-first system with Arabic bolted on (the cardinal sin here).
- Decorative, playful, or overly-styled type that undercuts seriousness.
- Too many weights/styles (noise; contradicts calm).
- Poor Arabic shaping, awkward diacritics, or a system-default Arabic font.
- Ambiguous numerals or non-tabular figures in data contexts.
- Trendy display faces that will date quickly (premium = timeless).

---

## 7. Deliverables for Claude Design (typography)

- A recommended **Arabic-first type system** (display + functional tiers), with the Arabic decision
  leading.
- Specimen of Arabic Fusha at headline, body, UI, and metric sizes.
- Proof of clean **bilingual** setting (Arabic with embedded Latin platform names/numerals).
- A type **hierarchy/scale** for website, reports, and diagnostics.
- Mobile/small-size Arabic legibility proof.

> If the typography is right — Arabic-first, premium, calm, precise — the brand will feel right
> even before colour and logo are settled, because in this restrained direction, type *is* the
> brand.
